TL;DR

Vinod Khosla advocates for the immediate deployment of AI-driven tutors, doctors, and agronomists integrated into India’s Aadhaar ecosystem to provide high-level expert services to the bottom half of the population at near-zero cost.

Revolutionizing Education via AI Tutors

  • Personalized Learning: Khosla argues that AI tutors are superior to human tutors because they use "knowledge tracing" to assess a student's specific gaps within minutes and tailor instruction accordingly.
  • Existing Scale: Through ck-12.org, over 400 million students globally and 4 million in India have already used these AI-based services.
  • Diksha 3.0: Khosla proposes upgrading India's current "Diksha" content library—which he describes as currently "unusable"—into an AI-first experience in partnership with Sarvam AI.
      • The system is already compatible with CBSE and National Education Policy standards.
      • Services are available in multiple Indic languages, including Hindi, Odia, and others.

Democratizing Healthcare with AI Doctors

  • Comprehensive Primary Care: AI can provide 24/7 expertise in disease management, mental health, physical therapy, and nutrition coaching at a level of comprehensiveness currently unavailable even in Western countries.
  • Solving the Doctor Shortage: Khosla notes that India cannot reach Western doctor-patient ratios through traditional means, even with a trillion dollars and decades of time; AI is the only viable way to scale resources.
  • The "Intern" Implementation Model: He suggests a 1–2 year transition where AI acts as an "MBBS graduate intern" overseen by human physicians before moving toward more autonomous operation.
      • The AI can diagnose, prescribe tests, and handle dialogue, only triaging to humans for physical exams or emergencies.

AI Agronomy for Rural Farmers

  • PhD-Level Expertise: The goal is to provide every farmer with a PhD-level agronomist available locally on their specific small plot of land.
  • Low Barrier to Entry: Recognizing literacy challenges, these systems are designed to function via voice and image recognition.
      • Farmers can simply speak to the AI or take pictures of their crops to receive expert advice.

The Infrastructure Proposal: AI as a Public Utility

  • Integration with Aadhaar/UPI: Khosla proposes that these AI services be integrated into the Aadhaar identity system, similar to how UPI revolutionized digital payments.
  • Non-Profit Delivery: He plans to build a "Section 8" non-profit company to build, operate, and eventually transfer these systems into the state-run Aadhaar ecosystem.
  • Localized Adaptation: The systems require iterative cycles to adapt to specific Indian conditions, local languages, and regional disease patterns.
