TL;DR

As AI automates rote tasks, organizations must transition from static job descriptions to fluid, skill-based roles while intentionally reinvesting saved time into high-value human relationships and strategic "unlearning."

AI Implementation and the "Top of License" Philosophy

Cassandra Pratt, CHRO of Progyny, emphasizes that AI transformation is not about replacing workers, but about redistributing tasks to enhance professional fulfillment and efficiency.

  • Work to the Top of Your License: This guiding principle, used particularly with Progyny’s clinicians, encourages employees to spend more time on complex, high-level intellectual tasks while delegating administrative burdens—like note-taking or data entry—to AI agents.
  • Tiered Rollout Strategy: Progyny introduced AI through a structured roadmap:
      • Personal Productivity: Initial focus on individual efficiency gains.
      • Custom Agents: Transitioning toward building specialized agents and sharing prompts across teams.
      • Scheduled Training: Clearly outlining what will be learned in specific quarters (e.g., Q1 vs. Q2) to prevent employee overwhelm.
  • The 5% Efficiency Goal: The company anticipates gaining 5% of staff time back initially through AI, with expectations for that number to grow to 15–20% as implementation matures.

The "Unlearn" Framework and Meeting Culture

Drawing on Barry O’Reilly’s book *Unlearn*, Progyny utilizes a "Start, Stop, Continue" framework to sharpen execution and reduce burnout.

  • The "Stop" Priority: Leaders identified operational items and project reflexes that did not move the business forward.
  • Meeting Discipline: Progyny implemented a "No Agenda, No Meeting" policy.
      • Agenda Requirements: Must include context, goals (brainstorming vs. decision-making), and participant roles.
      • Empowerment: Employees are permitted to decline meetings that lack a well-formed agenda.
  • Collaborative Problem-Solving: Pratt noted a shift in workplace behavior: instead of "dropping a problem" on a colleague’s desk, employees now use AI for preliminary research to bring more well-formed positions to subject matter experts.

Fluid Job Design and the Rise of the Generalist

The traditional, static job description is becoming obsolete in an era where AI-driven workflows evolve rapidly.

  • Skill-Based Fluidity: Job design is shifting from fixed responsibilities to matching specific employee skills with immediate business needs.
      • Dialogue: Leadership must maintain open communication to reassure employees that shifting job descriptions signify positive growth rather than role elimination.
  • The Generalist Advantage: Pratt argues that while specialists remain valuable, "generalists will be the kings of the AI era" because they can connect dots across silos and package AI-generated insights for various audiences.
  • Skills Mismatch: Pratt expressed skepticism about "fixing" the skills mismatch entirely, noting that the rapid pace of AI means training must become an ongoing, experiential process rather than a one-time fix.

Building "Superfans" Through Human Connection

As AI takes over technical work, Progyny is reinvesting human time into building deeper relationships with internal and external stakeholders.

  • Superfans Strategy: Employees are encouraged to use time saved by AI to understand their "audience" (clients, investors, or colleagues) more deeply.
  • Spotlighting Humanity: Progyny hosts "Spirit Weeks" and "Critical Impact Awards" to showcase employee "crafts"—ranging from painting and yoga to technical skills like Canva—to foster genuine human bonds that machines cannot replicate.
  • Intentional Engagement: Pratt recommends a "walking around" strategy for leaders to maintain physical human connection in a hybrid world, prioritizing active listening over transactional project updates.

Strategic Well-being and Future-Proof Benefits

Progyny views comprehensive benefits not as "add-ons," but as critical components of a high-performance culture.

  • Niche Support: The company pioneered pregnancy loss leave in 2021 and offers specific support for parents with infants in the NICU.
  • Catastrophic Leave: A flexible leave type designed to support employees during major life traumas that exceed standard 30-day or 90-day policies.
  • Financial ROI: Pratt warns companies against cutting well-being benefits, noting that neglecting mental and physical health leads to higher medical claims and lost productivity that far exceed the cost of the benefits themselves.

The 2026 midterm elections are trending toward a split government with Democrats favored to flip the House, while the Federal Reserve prepares for a "regime change" under Kevin Warsh focused on shrinking the $6 trillion balance sheet rather than immediate rate cuts.

The 2026 Midterm Landscape

  • Trump’s Approval Rating: Currently sits at 38%. Clifton notes that Trump’s approval on the economy went negative for the first time in April 2025 following tariff proposals, turning a former political asset into a liability.
  • The House of Representatives: Democrats only need a net gain of three seats to take control. Clifton predicts they will likely succeed, transitioning the midterms into a referendum on the incumbent.
  • The Senate Battle: Republicans currently hold a 53-47 majority. Democrats need a net gain of four seats for total control (or three if they hold the VP tie-breaker).
      • North Carolina: Likely to flip to Democrats due to a popular former Democratic governor running.
      • Maine: Market odds give Democrats a 60% chance to flip Susan Collins' seat, though her incumbency remains a formidable obstacle.
      • Ohio and Texas: Expected to be highly competitive and expensive races, though Republicans remain slight favorites to hold.
      • The "Powell Factor": Fed Chair Jerome Powell may delay his departure until after the midterms; if Democrats win the Senate, Trump would be forced to appoint a mainstream economist rather than a "MAGA" candidate for the role.

The "Warsh Fed" and Monetary Policy

  • Balance Sheet Focus: Kevin Warsh is prioritizing shrinking the Fed’s $6 trillion balance sheet over interest rate adjustments. The goal is to reduce the money supply—which leads goods inflation by approximately 15 months—to eventually allow for lower rates.
  • Privatizing Reserves: The strategy involves "privatizing" the Fed balance sheet by easing bank regulations (e.g., liquidity coverage ratios), encouraging private banks to hold more Treasuries and absorb the liquidity currently provided by the Fed.
  • Market Resilience: Despite expectations shifting from rate cuts to a possible 25-basis-point hike, the market has remained resilient because the broader economy is stronger than anticipated.

Tariffs and Fiscal Stimulus

  • Headwind to Tailwind: While tariffs were a market drag in early 2025, they have become a tailwind. The effective tariff rate dropped from 11% in October to 7% today.
  • Net Fiscal Impact: The U.S. has collected only $35 billion in net tariff revenue year-to-date while distributing $75 billion in corporate tax cuts and tariff refunds.
  • Currency Strategy: The next phase of "Trump 2.0" trade policy likely involves an aggressive push to lower the value of the U.S. dollar to boost the impact of domestic investment incentives.

The 2028 Presidential Outlook

  • Democratic Frontrunners: Gavin Newsom is the current betting favorite, followed by Pete Buttigieg, who is viewed as a "fighter" by the base. Other contenders include Governors Josh Shapiro (PA), Gretchen Whitmer (MI), and Wes Moore (MD).
  • Republican Field:
      • J.D. Vance: Has a significant head start because Trump is not restricting his campaign activities.
      • Marco Rubio: Seen as a "superstar" who can articulate Trump’s foreign policy to the establishment, though his standing is tied to the success of the Iran conflict.
      • Ron DeSantis: Remains highly active on national issues and is positioning himself for a populist run centered on affordability.

Emerging Risks: AI Backlash and Regional War

  • Data Centers as "The New Fracking": Clifton warns of a growing political backlash against AI data centers due to their massive consumption of land, water, and electricity.

      • Defense Shift: The conflict is accelerating a shift in investor preference toward "Defense Tech" (drones/software) over traditional large-cap hardware providers.

TL;DR: Arjun Bhatnagar, CEO of Cloaked, has raised $400 million to pioneer "data poisoning," a privacy strategy that uses masked, unique credentials for every transaction to disrupt the ability of AI and data brokers to track individual identities.

The Core Philosophy: Data Poisoning

  • The Vulnerability of a Single Number: Bhatnagar highlights that a single phone number can reveal an individual's Social Security number, address, family members, and credit card history because it acts as a universal identifier.
  • The "Poisoning" Strategy: Rather than just deleting old data, Cloaked "poisons the well" by providing users with thousands of unique phone numbers, emails, and credit cards.
      • Disrupting Identity: By giving different information to every merchant (e.g., Domino’s vs. Sephora), there is no clear consensus for surveillance systems to build a profile.
      • Contextual Relevance: Data brokers value current, contextual data; Cloaked renders this data useless by ensuring it doesn't map back to a central identity.
  • The Privacy Paradox: Bhatnagar notes that "people like to feel known, but they don't want to feel surveilled," aiming to balance personalization with safety.

Product Capabilities and Security Infrastructure

  • Three-Pillar Protection: The platform is designed to manage the full lifecycle of personal data:

1. Clean up the Past: Scanning the dark web and removing existing data.

2. Defend the Present: Using AI agents to screen calls; 40% of the millions of calls Cloaked handles daily are identified as AI scams.

3. Protect the Future: Generating real-time masked credentials (emails, phone numbers, credit cards, and soon physical addresses).

  • Technical Architecture: To prevent a mass breach, Cloaked does not use a single "multi-tenant" database.
      • Unique Infrastructure: Every user has their own unique database and infrastructure.
      • Zero-Knowledge Encryption: The company cannot access user data; even if the system is breached, an attacker would have to hack every individual user's encrypted database separately.
  • Financial Services Integration: Cloaked allows users to create merchant-specific credit cards with hard spending limits (e.g., a card exclusively for Spotify capped at $15/month).

Explosive Business Growth and Market Scale

  • Funding Milestones: The company has raised over $400 million, including a $375 million Series B in 2024.
  • User Adoption:
      • Consumer Base: Grown from 20,000 to nearly 400,000 paying subscribers in approximately one year.
      • Pricing Model: Primarily B2C, with users paying roughly $10/month or $100/year.
      • Enterprise Shift: Expanded from zero to over 50 enterprise accounts in four months, driven by employees wanting the tool for business use.
  • Strategic Partnerships:
      • NFLPA Partnership: Cloaked has a deal to provide privacy services to every NFL athlete to protect them from doxing, physical threats, and financial fraud.
      • Sports as a Standard: After working with the Chicago Cubs, Cloaked views professional sports as the "gold standard" for testing high-stakes security.

Founder Insights: High-Stakes Leadership

  • The $1.5M Marketing Gamble: In late 2023/early 2024, Cloaked was nearly out of cash. Bhatnagar decided to spend the final $1.5 million on performance marketing to prove the "story" of their growth. The gamble worked, leading to their massive Series B.
