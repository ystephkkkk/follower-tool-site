TL;DR

Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi outlines a strategic transition plan to transform Iran from a "North Korea of the region" into a secular democracy, projecting a $1 trillion economic opportunity for the U.S. and a definitive end to the current regime's regional destabilization.

The Transition Roadmap: The Iran Prosperity Project (IPP)

Prince Pahlavi and advisor Shervin Pishevar detailed a structured, multi-phase plan to stabilize the nation following the expected collapse of the current Islamic Republic.

  • The 100-Day Focus: The immediate priority is the Iran Prosperity Project (IPP), designed to stabilize the economy and infrastructure within the first three months of the regime's fall.
  • Structured Timeline:
      • Referendum: A four-month window to establish a transitional framework.
      • Constitutional Assembly: A six-month process to gather representatives and draft a new governing document.
      • Finalization: A 14-month window to finalize the constitution and hold the first free parliamentary and government elections.
  • Four Core Pillars: Any participating entity in the transition must adhere to four principles: territorial integrity, the strict separation of religion and state, equality under the rule of law, and the commitment to a democratic process.
  • Avoiding "De-Ba’athification" Errors: Unlike the Iraq war, the plan involves absorbing the current civilian bureaucracy and non-criminal military members to maintain national stability.

Economic Potential and Geopolitical Shift

The Prince emphasized that a democratic Iran would fundamentally reorder the global and regional economy.

  • Untapped Markets: Pahlavi described Iran as the "most untapped economic opportunity of the 21st century," estimating that business with the U.S. could generate $1 trillion in value within the first 10 years.
  • The "South Korea" Vision: Pahlavi argued that without the 1979 revolution, Iran would have been the region's South Korea; he intends to return the nation to that trajectory by opening it for global business.
  • Regional Stability: A free Iran would end the "Death to America" and "Death to Israel" ideologies, replacing state-sponsored terror with a partnership that Shervin Pishevar noted would create a massive "peace dividend" for the Middle East.
  • Human Capital: Pishevar highlighted the success of the Iranian diaspora (founders/executives at Uber, Google, and eBay) as a blueprint for the "brilliance" currently being suppressed within Iran’s 93-million-person population.

Governance and Pahlavi’s Role

Despite his royal title, Pahlavi positioned himself as a "bridge" rather than a permanent ruler.

  • Neutral Arbiter: Pahlavi stated his role is to manage the transition, not to seek personal power. He is indifferent to whether the people choose a Parliamentary Monarchy (similar to Sweden or Japan) or a Republic (similar to France or the U.S.).
  • The Ballot Box: He emphasized that the final form of government must be determined by a transparent, popular vote.
  • Relations with the U.S. Administration: Pahlavi confirmed he is communicating with the Trump administration via Steve Witkoff and members of Congress to ensure the U.S. supports the Iranian people's right to choose their own leaders.

Domestic Sentiment and Regime Defection

The discussion highlighted the growing disconnect between the Iranian people and the ruling clerics.

  • Military Defections: Shervin Pishevar reported that over 50,000 members of the military have already used secure channels to communicate with Pahlavi’s team regarding potential defections.
  • Pro-Western Sentiment: Pahlavi reminded the audience that after 9/11, Iran was the only country in the region where citizens held spontaneous candlelight vigils for American victims, signaling a deep-seated affinity for the West.
  • Regime Brutality: Pishevar noted the urgency of the transition, citing the recent massacre of over 43,000 peaceful protestors by the regime as evidence that the current leadership is a "murderous and evil" occupation force.
