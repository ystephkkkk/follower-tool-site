TL;DR

The era of international trust has been replaced by raw power dynamics (*rapports de force*), requiring Europe to urgently unify its capital markets and France to adopt a constitutional "golden rule" for debt to maintain global relevance.

European Sovereignty and the Tech Gap

  • The Single Most Important Lever: Capital Markets Union. Breton argues that Europe’s 450 million citizens have the world’s highest savings rate, but €300 billion of European savings flows to the US annually because the EU lacks unified investment instruments to scale innovative companies.
  • The "Industrial Data" Advantage: While the US dominated the era of personal data (GAFAM), Breton asserts the next revolution is Industrial Data. Europe remains more industrial than the US and possesses the world's largest reservoir of professional data, which will fuel the next generation of AI and algorithms.
  • ASML as a Benchmark: Breton dismisses the "myth" that Europe cannot produce tech giants, citing the Dutch firm ASML as the world’s most important tech company, holding a 95% market share in the lithography machines required to make advanced microchips.
  • The "Vassal" Mindset: He rejects the notion that Europe is a technological vassal, noting that the continent produces more Nobel prizes relative to its size than any other and simply needs the financial "fuel" to match its scientific output.

Regulating Big Tech and the Elon Musk Evolution

  • Musk’s Political Radicalization: Breton describes Elon Musk’s shift from a "deep Democrat" to "MAGA" as a radical transformation triggered specifically by the July 2024 assassination attempt on Donald Trump.
      • Early Impressions: Meeting Musk in 2013, Breton found him "weird" (obsessed with sun protection and wearing white gloves) but initially cooperative regarding European regulations.
  • The Logic of the DSA: Breton defends the Digital Services Act (DSA) as a necessary "steering wheel change."
      • Market Access: Just as a UK car must move its steering wheel to be sold in mainland Europe, tech platforms must adapt to EU laws on hate speech and child protection if they want access to 450 million consumers.
      • Systemic Risk: Platforms with over 45 million users are "systemic"; their algorithms can amplify social harm, necessitating stricter oversight.
  • US Visa Denial: Addressing reports that he is barred from the US, Breton clarifies he was labeled the "mastermind" behind the DSA by the US State Department. He views this as a political attempt to weaken Europe’s regulatory stance rather than a legal dispute.

Geopolitics: The "End of Trust"

  • January 20, 2025: Breton identifies the second Trump inauguration as the definitive "end of trust" in the Western world. The "pillar of trust" that governed the WTO, NATO alliances, and global contracts has collapsed.
  • Return of Imperial Logics: He observes that the US, Russia (under a "Nicolas II" mindset), and China (referencing ancient emperors) have returned to imperial power plays.
  • Europe’s Power Deficit: Europe was built on a "refusal of power" following the world wars. Breton argues the EU must now adopt the "attributes of power"—specifically military unification and economic leverage—to survive in a world where "partners" only respect those who can inflict mutual harm.

The French Fiscal Crisis and 2027

  • The Debt Divergence: Breton highlights a "lamentable" fiscal trajectory. When he left the Ministry of Finance in 2007, French debt was at 63% of GDP (similar to Germany). Today, Germany remains at ~60% while France has soared to 120%.
      • Lack of Investment: He notes that this debt was not used for long-term investment but for non-durable spending, unlike Germany which faced the same crises (Covid, Subprimes) but maintained discipline.
  • The Constitutional Solution: Breton advocates for a Constitutional "Golden Rule"—an "Union Sacrée" where all political parties agree to a deficit ceiling (similar to Germany’s 2011 reform). This would immediately lower interest rates and restore French credibility in the Eurogroup.
  • The 2027 Presidential Portrait: He describes the 2027–2032 term as the most critical since WWII.
      • Experience Matters: He argues for a leader with "white hair"—someone with a proven track record in complex crises who acts on experience and wisdom rather than "instinct or whims."
