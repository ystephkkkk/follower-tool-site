TL;DR

Supabase is an open-source, Postgres-backed database and developer tools platform founded in 2020 by Paul Copplestone, which grew from an initial open-source side project into a decacorn valued at $10B after raising a $500M round. The platform has scaled to 10 million developers across 60+ countries with 360 employees, and currently powers 60% to 90% of all AI agent-generated databases.

Background & Early Ventures

  • Paul Copplestone (Co-founder & CEO): Originally from New Zealand, grew up with an entrepreneur father, and spent only one brief stint in a corporate consultancy before dedicating his career entirely to startups.
  • Previous Ventures:
    • Startup #1: Served as CTO; did not find product-market fit, leading Paul to leave after two years over strategic disagreements with the CEO.
    • Startup #2: Incubated Supabase as a side project inside a different dev tools company for two years before officially launching it out into the market.
  • Why Postgres & Open Source:
    • Paul closely monitored Hacker News three times a day and noticed Postgres trending upward in popularity.
    • Chose open source due to personal philosophy and community-building values; monetization took about a year and a half to begin.

Launch & Developer Experience

  • Early Positioning: Initially tried a real-time Postgres engine, but growth took off only after rebranding and positioning as the "Open Source Firebase Alternative."
  • Initial Target Market: High-growth, venture-backed startups (specifically Y Combinator companies). Approximately over 60% of every YC batch now uses Supabase, though almost no one trusted them during their own batch.
  • Time to Value Metrics:
    • Measured AWS RDS instance spin-up time at around 8.5 minutes.
    • Set a strict internal goal to drop their own setup time under a minute, which eventually reached roughly 5 seconds.
    • Transitioned the developer experience from a visual dashboard/SQL editor to CLIs, MCPs, and Git-backed declarative schemas designed for AI agents.

The AI Era & Agent-Driven Scale

  • Explosive Growth (2024–2026):
    • Growth accelerated dramatically with the rise of AI builders and "vibe coding" tools like Bolt, Lovable, Claude Code, and Codex.
    • Monthly user metrics jumped from 6.5 million to 10 million developers, with conversion rates and user activation accelerating simultaneously despite zero traditional marketing spend.
  • Supabase for Platforms: Built to support platforms like Lovable and Bolt; enables the automated spin-up of millions of databases. Also adapted for enterprise innovation labs to manage and monitor large volumes of databases.

Company Operations & Culture

  • Remote-First Organization: Employs 360 people across more than 60 countries with zero physical offices.
  • AI-Driven Documentation: Because every decision over the past six years has been fully documented in writing across Slack and Notion, their internal AI agents possess institutional memory dating back to day one.
  • Funding & Valuation: Raised a massive series of rounds, culminating in a $500 million funding round at a valuation exceeding $10 billion.

Predictions & Forward Calls

  • Paul Copplestone: 60% to 90% of all new database launches are now triggered by AI agents.
  • Paul Copplestone: The next major wave of dev tool opportunity will shift from the "build stage" (prompting and generation) to the "operate stage" (self-driving, automated database management, security patches, and uptime maintenance).

TL;DR

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang dismissed extreme warnings about artificial intelligence as "complete nonsense," arguing that AI is driving unprecedented productivity and net job creation rather than eliminating human labor. During an exclusive interview with Axios' Mike Allen in Fort Worth, Texas, Huang also discussed U.S.-China technology competition, the rise of open-source models, and why Wall Street fundamentally misunderstands the current infrastructure buildout.

Geopolitical Competition & Trade with China

  • State of China Sales: Huang stated that Nvidia's sales to China are approximately zero and advised investors to expect zero sales unless the U.S. and Chinese governments permit a return to the market.
  • Chinese AI Models: Praised models like DeepSeek, Kimi, and GLM as excellent tools that advance the global industry. Huang rejected the misconception that Chinese models contain backdoors, noting they operate safely within isolated "sandboxes" and harnesses.
  • Open vs. Closed Models: Argued that while closed models (like Anthropic and OpenAI) are fantastic for everyday use, open-source models are foundational for science, cybersecurity, and national security because transparency allows the global community to inspect and harden them.
  • Government Regulation: Emphasized that regulation should focus on specific dual-use applications (such as autonomous vehicles and medicine) rather than restricting the underlying technology itself.

Market Misconceptions & Wall Street's "CAPEX" Blindspot

  • The Stock Market Drop: Explained that Wall Street panicked over recent Chinese open-source releases (similar to the DeepSeek reaction), incorrectly assuming free AI hurts hardware demand. Huang maintained that "free AI is great for hardware, chips, and data centers" because better models drive higher usage.
  • The New CAPEX Reality: Acknowledged that the new IT industry requires significantly heavier capital expenditure (CAPEX) to build out the intelligence layer (AI factories and data centers), but argued this infrastructure buildout is industrially driven rather than cyclical or seasonal.
  • Lack of Demand Bubble: Stated that the semiconductor industry needs to be 5 to 10 times larger than it is today, with current shortages across memory, storage, optical interconnects, and TSMC foundries.
  • The AI Flywheel: Noted that an inflection point has been reached where AI is both useful and profitable—evidenced by companies gladly paying hundreds of millions for coding agents that dramatically increase productivity.

The Jobs Narrative & Economic Reality

  • Net Job Creation: Refuted predictions that AI will eliminate half of American jobs, presenting evidence of rising headcounts:
    • Radiologists: Increased by roughly 20% due to automated scans allowing doctors to see significantly more patients.
    • Paralegals: Increased by roughly 10% as automated case handling allowed firms to clear massive backlogs.
    • Manufacturing: Increased by some 50% over the last several years to build AI data centers and infrastructure.
  • Closing the Technology Divide: Highlighted AI as the first major technology that closes the technology divide, acting as a personal coach and tutor for anyone regardless of background.
  • Small Corporate Footprint: Shared that despite Nvidia's massive valuation, it employs only 50,000 people and aims to keep headcount "as small as possible" (projecting roughly 75,000 in a decade) compared to tech giants with six-figure headcounts.

Robotics, Agents, and Future Timelines

  • The Robotic ChatGPT Moment: Declared that the "ChatGPT moment" for robotics has already arrived, noting that robots can now reason through complex, multi-step physical tasks when given natural language prompts.
  • The Agentic Era: Predicted that the computing landscape is shifting from 100 million active human users to a future dominated by 100 billion to a trillion software agents running continuously and consuming vast amounts of computer resources.

Predictions & Forward Calls

Jason Hardy (VP of Storage Technology at NVIDIA) explains how the shift toward agentic AI requires enterprise storage to evolve from a static system of record into a dynamic system of context and a new data plane. This transformation relies on AI-ready data platforms, specialized memory tiers for KV cache, and co-designed NVIDIA technologies like Vera and BlueField-4 STX working alongside storage partners.

The Three Pillars of Enterprise Data & Agentic Demand

  • Structured Data: Comprising small volumes from ERP systems, SQL databases, and business systems where core operational decisions are made.
  • Unstructured Data: Representing the lion's share of enterprise volume—including home directories, SharePoint, OneDrive, Google Docs, NAS shares, PDFs, video, and audio—noted as the hardest to process due to diverse data types.
  • Agent Context (The New Category): Encompassing KV cache, agent memory, scratchpads, to-do lists, and embeddings generated as agents consume information and execute tasks; some agents operate for hours while others persist for years.
  • New Operational Paradigm: Storage must transition from stateless, short-lived interactions to handling long-running, 24/7 sessions where tens to thousands of agents iterate over blended structured and unstructured datasets at speeds vastly exceeding human reading rates.

Data Preparation & The AI Data Platform

  • Overcoming Enterprise Hallucinations: High-profile failures (Air Canada, UnitedHealth, Google, Deloitte) are fundamentally data preparation issues rather than purely AI model flaws.
  • Complex Ingestion Pipeline:
    • Multimodal Ingestion: Processing text, tables, and imagery within documents like PDFs concurrently.
    • Cleaning & Enrichment: Removing duplication, normalizing data, stripping out PII and sensitive data, and attaching descriptive metadata.
    • Chunking & Vectorization: Breaking data into semantic units, embedding them into vector databases, indexing, and governing them.
  • In-Place AI Data Platforms: NVIDIA is collaborating with partners like Dell, NetApp, IBM, and HPE to embed AI-ready capabilities directly into enterprise storage systems at the time of creation.
  • NVIDIA Blueprints: Utilizing the RAG blueprint to streamline vectorization and embedding, and the VSS (Video Search and Summarization) blueprint to help agents understand video context directly within the storage system.

Memory Hierarchies and KV Cache Innovation

  • Memory Tiering Architecture:
    • G1 (HBM): Fastest memory directly inside the GPU, holding active KV cache and model weights.
    • G2 (System Memory): Tightly coupled memory collaborating with HBM for model staging and spillover.
    • G3 (Scratch): NVMe disk inside each server for operating systems.
    • G3.5 (CMX): A new dedicated tier introduced by NVIDIA specifically for KV cache management, sitting between high-performance storage and GPU memory.
    • G4 (High-Performance Storage): Traditional high-performance storage, object storage, and enterprise storage where models live and checkpointing occurs.
  • KV Cache Efficiency: CMX stores and serves prefill and decode stage token computations so GPUs spend zero time recomputing existing cached data, maximizing token-per-second output across the AI factory.

Hardware Foundations & Co-Design

  • Vera CPU Integration: Designed to take bits, bytes, and packets off the wire and feed them to the GPU with 3x memory bandwidth and best-in-class single-threaded performance, directly benefiting storage workloads.
  • NVIDIA Vera BlueField-4 STX Platform: An opinionated storage design acting as the foundation for the AI factory, combining NVIDIA software, Vera/BlueField-4 STX hardware, and storage partner ecosystems.
  • Horizontal & Vertical Co-Design: Integrating network (Spectrum-X), compute (Vera), GPUs (Rubin/RTX Pro), and storage platforms to scale agentic AI safely while maintaining enterprise trust, security, compliance, and hybrid on-prem/cloud governance.

TL;DR

Women spend 25% more of their lives in poor health than men—disproportionately during prime working years (ages 20 to 60)—driving a $1 trillion annual global economic loss. A report by the World Economic Forum and the McKinsey Health Institute outlines three cost-effective, high-return care delivery interventions in pregnancy, screening, and mental health that can close this gap and deliver a 3x to 6x return on investment.

The Women's Health Gap: Scale and Economic Impact

  • Staggering Health Disparity: Women spend 25% more time in poor health than men, a gap historically misattributed to end-of-life longevity but actually most pronounced during prime working years between the ages of 20 and 60.
  • Massive Economic Loss: This health gap takes women out of the workforce, resulting in a lost trillion dollars of economic opportunity each year.
  • Proportionate Burden of Non-Gender-Specific Conditions: While pregnancy is a major physiological stress, the majority of the health gap ties to conditions impacting both sexes disproportionately or differently, such as heart disease.
  • Heart Disease Fatality Rate: Women are 50% more likely to die in the year following a heart attack than men, largely due to a medical history of treating female biology as "atypical."

Three High-Impact, Cost-Effective Care Delivery Interventions

  • 1. Breast Arterial Calcification via Mammograms:
    • Over 200 million women get mammograms annually. These images can simultaneously detect breast arterial calcification (calcium buildup in arteries), indicating future cardiovascular event risk.
    • Standardizing and annotating existing mammograms costs practically nothing additional, yet enables early cardiology referrals to avert major downstream heart events.
  • 2. Pregnancy Complications as Cardiac Early Warnings:
    • Hypertensive disorders like preeclampsia and gestational diabetes make women twice as likely to develop serious heart disease later in life.
    • Currently, health systems monitor these conditions during pregnancy but abandon proactive follow-up postpartum, allowing heart disease to surface aggressively decades later.
    • Financial ROI: Improving follow-up and long-term care costs $400 to $450 per woman, far lower than the $100,000+ cost of treating a major cardiovascular event. In the US alone, this averts $6 million to $25 million in costs, representing a 3x to 5x return on investment.
  • 3. Perinatal Depression Screening and Integration:
    • Postpartum depression impacts roughly 20% of women, but focusing solely on the postpartum period is too narrow. Evidence shows manifestation often begins earlier in pregnancy, requiring a holistic "perinatal depression" approach across the entire pregnancy and up to one year post-delivery.
    • Leveraging clinically validated screening tools integrated directly into routine prenatal care de-stigmatizes the condition and protects the health of both mother and baby.

Systemic Barriers and the Role of AI in Healthcare

  • Siloed Health Systems: Connecting diagnostic data across separate medical departments and budgets is challenging, which explains why these common-sense care integrations have not happened automatically.
  • The Data Foundation for AI: While artificial intelligence garners massive enthusiasm, AI models are only as good as their underlying data. Historically, datasets (even during COVID-19) frequently failed to code for sex as a biological variable.
  • Clinical Decision Support: True technological advancement requires clinical software tools to recognize female-specific presentation patterns (such as different heart attack symptoms) immediately upon identifying a patient as female.

Personal Insights and Medical Education Blind Spots

  • Diagnostic Biases: Medical schools historically failed to teach sex-based differences, falsely training clinicians that women are merely "smaller men" requiring only adjusted medication dosages.
  • Research and Diagnostics Discrepancy: McKinsey partner research shows that conditions unique to women are diagnosed much less frequently than male-specific conditions—a disparity widened when male providers perform the diagnosis.
  • Endometriosis Neglect: Endometriosis impacts 1 in 10 women (the exact same prevalence as diabetes), yet while diabetes has over 500 drugs in clinical development, endometriosis has zero approved drugs and only about 20 drugs in clinical development.
