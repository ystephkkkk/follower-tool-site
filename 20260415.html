TL;DR

Liquid AI is pioneering a new class of "Liquid Foundation Models" (LFMs) that are two orders of magnitude smaller than traditional cloud-based models, enabling high-performance, proactive AI agents to run entirely offline on edge devices like AI PCs.

Architectural Innovation: Liquid Neural Networks (LNNs)

  • Brain-Inspired Computation: Liquid AI moved away from standard transformer architectures to create LNNs based on how neurons exchange information in nature.
      • Efficiency Gains: The company aims to shrink models from the trillion-parameter regime used in the cloud to a 100M to 10B parameter range without sacrificing quality.
      • Alternative Primitives: Unlike transformers that rely almost exclusively on matrix multiplication, LFMs explore diverse mathematical operators to optimize for memory and speed.
  • Hardware-in-the-Loop Design: The team uses a meta-AI system to search for the most efficient computational graph for a specific processor (CPU, NPU, or GPU).
      • Performance Metrics: This approach targets a memory footprint as small as 1GB while achieving latencies as low as 20 milliseconds for large language model (LLM) tasks.

The Shift to Proactive Edge AI

  • Reactive vs. Proactive Agents: Current AI often waits for a prompt (reactive); Liquid AI enables "proactive agents" that run continuously in the background to perform tasks on a user’s behalf.
  • On-Device Advantages:
      • Privacy and Security: By running models locally on Ryzen AI PCs, users can process sensitive data (financials, health records, or secret projects) in "air-gapped" environments.
      • Latency and Reliability: Local execution eliminates the need for an internet connection and avoids the latency spikes of cloud-based APIs.
      • Multimodality: LFMs are designed to handle vision, text, and audio—noting that low latency is particularly critical for natural audio interactions.

Power Efficiency and Sustainability

  • NPU Optimization: Liquid AI works closely with AMD to optimize workloads for Neural Processing Units (NPUs), which provide high-compute throughput (50-60 TOPs) at significantly lower power than traditional chips.
  • Sustainability: By "juicing out" maximum intelligence per watt at the algorithmic level, Liquid AI addresses the global energy crisis created by the massive power demands of traditional generative AI data centers.
  • Societal Impact: Mark Papermaster highlights that driving down the cost and power requirements of AI is a form of "democratization," making advanced intelligence accessible to regions or industries without massive infrastructure.

Ecosystem and the Future of Co-Design

  • Open Source Commitment: Liquid AI releases its models as "open weights," reporting over 1 million downloads per week as of early 2024.
  • Market Scale: Ramin Hasani estimates that out of the 35 billion processors manufactured annually, approximately 2 billion (across phones, laptops, and cars) are currently capable of hosting LFMs.
  • Hardware-Software Co-Design: The ultimate vision is a "holistic design" where the foundation model layer is so deeply integrated into the silicon that the chip itself is inherently "agent-aware."

TL;DR

The Trump administration marked Tax Day by highlighting the "Working Families Tax Cut Act," which delivers historic relief to middle-class Americans and rural communities, while simultaneously announcing the launch of the first-ever USDA Office of Seafood to integrate fishermen into federal agricultural support programs.

Economic Relief and Tax Implementation

  • Broad Tax Savings: The administration reported that 53 million filers have claimed at least one new tax cut, with the average refund this season exceeding $3,400.
  • Specific Provisions:
      • No Tax on Tips/Overtime: Over 6 million filers claimed "no tax on tips," and 25 million claimed "no tax on overtime," resulting in average savings of $7,000 for service workers.
      • Senior Relief: 51 million seniors (88% of those on Social Security) will now pay no tax on their Social Security benefits.
      • Standard Deduction: Over 100 million filers utilized the now-permanent double standard deduction.
  • Capital Investment: The act made permanent 100% immediate expensing for capital-intensive business investments, specifically aiding farmers and small businesses.

Revitalizing Rural America and Agriculture

  • The "Death Tax": Secretary Brooke Rollins confirmed the act averted the sunset of the death tax exemption, protecting two million family farms from liquidation.
  • Direct Rural Investment:
      • Rural Opportunity Zones: $100 billion is being driven into rural investment through Opportunity Zones, shifting the program's focus from urban to heartland communities.
      • Health Transformation: $50 billion is committed to the Rural Health Transformation program, overseen by Dr. Oz.
  • Commodity Price Protection: Reference prices for major commodities (corn, soybeans, wheat) will increase by 10% to 21%, providing up to $13 billion in additional revenue protection starting this October.
  • Trade Deficit Reduction: The administration reported cutting the inherited $50 billion agricultural trade deficit by 40% through 18 new trade deals.

The Historic Launch of the USDA Office of Seafood

  • "Farmers of the Sea": Secretary Rollins announced the first-ever USDA Office of Seafood, officially recognizing commercial fishermen as food producers rather than just resource harvesters.
  • Integration of Services: The office will provide fishermen access to USDA tools previously reserved for land farmers, including:
      • Risk management and insurance tools.
      • Marketing support and federal financing.
      • Disaster and weather incident relief.
  • Regulatory and Economic Protection:
      • Inspection Standards: Officials noted that currently, only 0.1% of imported seafood is inspected; the new office aims to protect domestic markets from "toxic" imported fish.
      • Defunding Offshore Wind: Fourth-generation lobsterman Dustin Delano praised the administration for eliminating subsidies for offshore wind projects that threaten traditional fishing grounds.

Federal Efficiency and Regulatory Reform

  • Permitting Acceleration: Secretary Doug Burgum highlighted a massive reduction in bureaucracy, reporting that Environmental Impact Statements (EIS) that previously took four years are now being completed in 24 days while still following the law.
  • Eliminating "Bloat": Secretary Rollins identified several taxpayer-funded studies (e.g., studies on transgender mice and "racism in the pest business") that were defunded to refocus the USDA on core agricultural production.
  • Energy Independence and Inputs:
      • Fertilizer Crisis: Secretary Rollins reported that fertilizer prices have risen 40% in the last month; the administration is engaging DOJ and fertilizer CEOs to prevent price gouging.
      • Natural Gas Advantage: Secretary Burgum noted that because the U.S. is a net exporter of natural gas (the primary component of nitrogen fertilizer), the administration intends to build domestic infrastructure to ensure American farmers have the world’s lowest fertilizer costs.

The most effective leaders act as "junction makers," fostering innovation by creating unexpected connections between disciplines, listening deeply to practitioners, and embracing serendipity over rigid master plans.

The Curator as "Junction Maker" and Enabler

  • Defining the Role: Hans Ulrich Obrist describes curation not as gatekeeping, but as being a "junction maker"—creating situations and unexpected connections between people, ideas, and artworks.
  • The Power of Listening: Creativity begins with listening rather than imposing a framework.
      • Artist Advocacy: Obrist views his role as an "enabler" who helps artists realize dreams that the traditional art world structure might otherwise prohibit.
      • The Studio Visit: Obrist has conducted daily studio visits since 1986, emphasizing that the best insights come from long-term, direct engagement with creators.
  • The "Unrealized Project" Inquiry: A core part of Obrist’s methodology is asking every collaborator about their unrealized projects.
      • Categories of Failure: Projects often remain unrealized because they are too big, too time-intensive, censored, or "self-censored" (ideas the creator didn't dare to try).
      • Untapped Potential: These "dreams" often sit outside an artist's daily routine and represent their most innovative thinking.

Innovation Through Multisensory and Digital Experiences

  • Extending Engagement: To move beyond the "few seconds" visitors typically spend in front of an artwork, the Serpentine creates multisensory environments.
      • Example: A Peter Doig exhibition transformed a gallery into a "listening space," encouraging visitors to stay for hours and return repeatedly.
  • Technology as a Lead Medium: The Serpentine established a dedicated technology department to produce "Future Art Ecosystems."
      • Gaming and Scale: A collaboration with *Fortnite* for a KAWS exhibition attracted 150 million virtual visitors in two weeks, successfully bringing a younger generation into the physical gallery.
      • Ethical AI: Artists like Holly Herndon and Refik Anadol are used to make "invisible" AI datasets visible and audible, demonstrating how data can be sourced and utilized ethically.
  • Bridging Disciplines: The Serpentine Pavilion project (now in its 26th year) invites international architects who have never built in the UK to create temporary structures, blending the discourses of art and architecture.

Long-Term Thinking vs. Short-Termism

  • Continuous Evolution: Obrist argues against the "short-termist deadlines" of the modern world.
      • The "do it" Project: An exhibition based on artist-written instructions that has been evolving and learning for 33 years.
      • Institutional Re-invention: The Serpentine adds new departments (e.g., Ecology, Technology) to its organizational "organigram" to stay relevant to societal shifts.
  • Mondialité: Following the philosophy of Édouard Glissant, Obrist advocates for "mondialité"—a global dialogue that resists the homogenizing forces of globalization while remaining deeply anchored in local culture.
  • The Polyphony of Centers: The art world has moved from a single center (Paris or New York) to a "polyphony" of global hubs, fueled by private foundations and artist-run initiatives.

Business Lessons from the Art World

  • The Art of the Pivot: Artists excel at navigating the "unknowable" and pivoting when faced with uncertainty—skills that are increasingly critical for modern CEOs.
  • Artists in the Boardroom: Obrist advocates for "Artist Placement Groups," suggesting that every corporation or government entity should have an artist-in-residence or an artist on the board to challenge traditional thinking.
  • Extreme Productivity and Rituals: Obrist shares his unconventional methods for managing a global workload:
      • The Night Producer: He employs a "night assistant" to handle correspondence and editing while he sleeps, ensuring a 24-hour productive cycle.
      • The Brutally Early Club: A ritual started in London to hold improvised meetings at 6:30 AM to bypass the rigid scheduling of the business world.

The Mechanics of "Hard" World-Building

  • The "Wrong Math" Spreadsheet: To maintain internal consistency, Weir created a spreadsheet specifically for "wrong math." This calculated how the alien species (the Eridians) would have over-fueled their ships because they lacked a discovery of relativity and relied on Newtonian physics.
  • Special Relativity as a Narrative Limit: The maximum distance for the Astrophage infection (eight light-years) was derived by calculating how long the organism could survive before radiating away its heat energy, adjusted for its own inertial reference frame.

  • Environmental Constraints: Weir started with the exoplanet 40 Eridani A b. Because the planet is close to its star (a 46-day year), he deduced it needed a thick atmosphere and a massive magnetic field to keep liquid water at 200°C without boiling away.

  • Data Dependency: Lagarde emphasizes that the ECB is not currently "predicating" a specific direction for interest rates. Any policy moves will be determined by whether incoming data suggests shocks are "short-lived/see-through" or "long-lasting."

  • Coordination with Fiscal Policy: Lagarde urges political leaders to ensure fiscal support is "Targeted, Temporary, Transparent, and Tailored" (the T-T-T-T framework) to avoid working against monetary price stability.
