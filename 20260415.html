TL;DR

Liquid AI is pioneering a new class of "Liquid Foundation Models" (LFMs) that are two orders of magnitude smaller than traditional cloud-based models, enabling high-performance, proactive AI agents to run entirely offline on edge devices like AI PCs.

Architectural Innovation: Liquid Neural Networks (LNNs)

  • Brain-Inspired Computation: Liquid AI moved away from standard transformer architectures to create LNNs based on how neurons exchange information in nature.
      • Efficiency Gains: The company aims to shrink models from the trillion-parameter regime used in the cloud to a 100M to 10B parameter range without sacrificing quality.
      • Alternative Primitives: Unlike transformers that rely almost exclusively on matrix multiplication, LFMs explore diverse mathematical operators to optimize for memory and speed.
  • Hardware-in-the-Loop Design: The team uses a meta-AI system to search for the most efficient computational graph for a specific processor (CPU, NPU, or GPU).
      • Performance Metrics: This approach targets a memory footprint as small as 1GB while achieving latencies as low as 20 milliseconds for large language model (LLM) tasks.

The Shift to Proactive Edge AI

  • Reactive vs. Proactive Agents: Current AI often waits for a prompt (reactive); Liquid AI enables "proactive agents" that run continuously in the background to perform tasks on a user’s behalf.
  • On-Device Advantages:
      • Privacy and Security: By running models locally on Ryzen AI PCs, users can process sensitive data (financials, health records, or secret projects) in "air-gapped" environments.
      • Latency and Reliability: Local execution eliminates the need for an internet connection and avoids the latency spikes of cloud-based APIs.
      • Multimodality: LFMs are designed to handle vision, text, and audio—noting that low latency is particularly critical for natural audio interactions.

Power Efficiency and Sustainability

  • NPU Optimization: Liquid AI works closely with AMD to optimize workloads for Neural Processing Units (NPUs), which provide high-compute throughput (50-60 TOPs) at significantly lower power than traditional chips.
  • Sustainability: By "juicing out" maximum intelligence per watt at the algorithmic level, Liquid AI addresses the global energy crisis created by the massive power demands of traditional generative AI data centers.
  • Societal Impact: Mark Papermaster highlights that driving down the cost and power requirements of AI is a form of "democratization," making advanced intelligence accessible to regions or industries without massive infrastructure.

Ecosystem and the Future of Co-Design

  • Open Source Commitment: Liquid AI releases its models as "open weights," reporting over 1 million downloads per week as of early 2024.
  • Market Scale: Ramin Hasani estimates that out of the 35 billion processors manufactured annually, approximately 2 billion (across phones, laptops, and cars) are currently capable of hosting LFMs.
  • Hardware-Software Co-Design: The ultimate vision is a "holistic design" where the foundation model layer is so deeply integrated into the silicon that the chip itself is inherently "agent-aware."

On Tax Day, the Trump administration reported record-breaking tax refunds and significant participation in signature tax cuts (tips, overtime, and senior deductions), while detailing a "maximum pressure" economic strategy against Iran and plans for a 250-foot Triumphal Arch to mark the nation's 250th anniversary.

Tax Season Performance and Individual Relief

  • Record Refunds: The average tax refund this season exceeded $3,400, the largest in U.S. history.
      • Participation: More than 53 million filers (approx. 45% of all returns) claimed at least one of the administration's signature tax cuts.
      • Standard Deduction: 105 million filers utilized the permanently doubled standard deduction.
  • Signature Policy Impact:
      • No Tax on Overtime: 25 million filers claimed this deduction, with an average savings of $3,100.
      • No Tax on Tips: 6 million filers saved an average of $7,100.
      • Senior Deductions: 30 million seniors claimed enhanced deductions averaging $7,500.
      • Auto Loans: Over 1 million filers claimed interest deductions on American-made cars, averaging $1,800.
  • Trump Accounts: Five million families have opened "Trump accounts" for children to promote financial literacy, with 1.2 million eligible for a $1,000 seed bonus.

Small Business and Industrial Growth

  • Pass-Through Deductions: Small Business Administrator Kelly Loeffler confirmed that the 20% small business pass-through deduction is now permanent, providing an average savings of $7,000 for 12 million businesses.
  • Job Creation: The administration credits these tax policies with providing the "rocket fuel" to create one million new jobs, citing examples of domestic manufacturers expanding shifts from one day a week to six.
  • Capital Investment: 100% expensing for equipment and R&D is being credited for a resurgence in "Made in America" manufacturing and the construction of new industrial facilities.

Energy and Foreign Policy

  • Gas Prices: Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent expressed optimism that gas prices will return to the $3.00 range between June and September, contingent on the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.
  • Iran Sanctions: The administration announced "Operation Economic Fury," a maximum-pressure campaign involving:
      • Secondary Sanctions: Warnings issued to Chinese banks regarding the processing of Iranian money.
      • Oil Blockades: A refusal to renew general licenses for Russian or Iranian oil.
      • Asset Freezes: Requests to GCC neighbors to investigate and freeze funds belonging to the IRGC leadership.
  • Pakistan Mediation: Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt clarified that Pakistan remains the sole mediator in ongoing ceasefire negotiations with Iran, emphasizing that while talks are productive, no official deal has been signed.

Administration and National Heritage

  • Personnel Updates: Todd Blanche remains the Acting Attorney General. The administration emphasized that accountability for the "weaponization of justice" under the previous term is a top priority for the next permanent appointee.
  • Federal Reserve: The administration is moving forward with the confirmation of Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair, despite reported opposition from some Republican senators.
  • 250th Anniversary: The Department of the Interior will submit plans for a 250-foot "United States Triumphal Arch" to be built on Columbia Island in the Potomac River. Construction is slated to begin this year to commemorate the 250th anniversary of American independence.

The Curator as "Junction Maker" and Enabler

  • Defining the Role: Hans Ulrich Obrist describes curation not as gatekeeping, but as being a "junction maker"—creating situations and unexpected connections between people, ideas, and artworks.
  • The Power of Listening: Creativity begins with listening rather than imposing a framework.
      • Artist Advocacy: Obrist views his role as an "enabler" who helps artists realize dreams that the traditional art world structure might otherwise prohibit.
      • The Studio Visit: Obrist has conducted daily studio visits since 1986, emphasizing that the best insights come from long-term, direct engagement with creators.
  • The "Unrealized Project" Inquiry: A core part of Obrist’s methodology is asking every collaborator about their unrealized projects.
      • Categories of Failure: Projects often remain unrealized because they are too big, too time-intensive, censored, or "self-censored" (ideas the creator didn't dare to try).
      • Untapped Potential: These "dreams" often sit outside an artist's daily routine and represent their most innovative thinking.

Innovation Through Multisensory and Digital Experiences

  • Extending Engagement: To move beyond the "few seconds" visitors typically spend in front of an artwork, the Serpentine creates multisensory environments.
      • Example: A Peter Doig exhibition transformed a gallery into a "listening space," encouraging visitors to stay for hours and return repeatedly.
  • Technology as a Lead Medium: The Serpentine established a dedicated technology department to produce "Future Art Ecosystems."
      • Gaming and Scale: A collaboration with *Fortnite* for a KAWS exhibition attracted 150 million virtual visitors in two weeks, successfully bringing a younger generation into the physical gallery.
      • Ethical AI: Artists like Holly Herndon and Refik Anadol are used to make "invisible" AI datasets visible and audible, demonstrating how data can be sourced and utilized ethically.
  • Bridging Disciplines: The Serpentine Pavilion project (now in its 26th year) invites international architects who have never built in the UK to create temporary structures, blending the discourses of art and architecture.

Long-Term Thinking vs. Short-Termism

  • Continuous Evolution: Obrist argues against the "short-termist deadlines" of the modern world.
      • The "do it" Project: An exhibition based on artist-written instructions that has been evolving and learning for 33 years.
      • Institutional Re-invention: The Serpentine adds new departments (e.g., Ecology, Technology) to its organizational "organigram" to stay relevant to societal shifts.
  • Mondialité: Following the philosophy of Édouard Glissant, Obrist advocates for "mondialité"—a global dialogue that resists the homogenizing forces of globalization while remaining deeply anchored in local culture.
  • The Polyphony of Centers: The art world has moved from a single center (Paris or New York) to a "polyphony" of global hubs, fueled by private foundations and artist-run initiatives.

Business Lessons from the Art World

  • The Art of the Pivot: Artists excel at navigating the "unknowable" and pivoting when faced with uncertainty—skills that are increasingly critical for modern CEOs.
  • Artists in the Boardroom: Obrist advocates for "Artist Placement Groups," suggesting that every corporation or government entity should have an artist-in-residence or an artist on the board to challenge traditional thinking.
  • Extreme Productivity and Rituals: Obrist shares his unconventional methods for managing a global workload:
      • The Night Producer: He employs a "night assistant" to handle correspondence and editing while he sleeps, ensuring a 24-hour productive cycle.
      • The Brutally Early Club: A ritual started in London to hold improvised meetings at 6:30 AM to bypass the rigid scheduling of the business world.

TL;DR

As AI workloads transition from "spiky" manual prompts to continuous, 24/7 agentic workflows, traditional compute metrics like FLOPs-per-dollar are becoming obsolete. The only metric that accurately captures total cost of ownership (TCO) and profitability is Cost per Token, which accounts for end-to-end efficiencies across power, cooling, hardware utilization, and software optimization.

The Evolution of the "Intelligence Factory"

  • The Token as an Atomic Unit: Much like the electron was the atomic unit of the industrial revolution, the token is the atomic unit of the AI era. Data centers are transitioning from general-purpose storage and compute hubs into "intelligence factories" producing tokens.
  • Shift in Usage Patterns:
      • From Spiky to Flatline: Nader Khalil (NVIDIA) notes that early AI usage was "peaky" (single questions). With the rise of reasoning models and 24/7 autonomous agents, usage has "flatlined" at maximum capacity.
      • Escape Velocity: The industry has hit a point where demand for intelligence is effectively unlimited, outpacing the growth of raw compute deployment.
  • The "Inference Iceberg": Looking only at the sticker price of a GPU or cost-per-hour is a "surface-level" mistake. True value lies deeper in the stack, specifically in how many tokens can be generated per megawatt of power.

Why Power is the Primary Driver of TCO

  • Dominant Opex: Dr. Gerro Prinsloo emphasizes that once an AI factory is built, operational expenses are dominated by energy usage. Any watt of electricity that does not reach the chip to produce a token is wasted capital.
  • The Inefficiency Gap:
      • PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness): While top-tier hyperscalers operate with a ~10% overhead (1.1 PUE), less efficient data centers can reach 100% overhead (2.0 PUE).
      • Margin Erosion: A factory owner unaware of energy leaks (transportation losses, cooling inefficiencies) will inadvertently misprice their tokens and lose market competitiveness.
  • Levelized Cost of Intelligence: Borrowing from the energy industry’s "Levelized Cost of Energy" (LCOE), AI operators must calculate the total capital investment plus lifetime opex divided by the total tokens produced over the asset's life.

Generational Gains: Blackwell vs. Hopper

  • Beyond FLOPs: Traditional metrics show the Blackwell architecture offers a ~2x improvement in FLOPs per dollar. However, when measured by the actual product (tokens), the improvement is exponentially higher.
  • 35x Cost Improvement: NVIDIA’s generational shift from Hopper to Blackwell delivers a 35x lower cost per million tokens.
  • 50x Power Efficiency: In a power-constrained world, the most critical ratio is tokens per second per megawatt, where Blackwell performs 50x higher than previous generations.
  • Bending the Pareto Curve: The combination of the Vera Rubin architecture and liquid cooling allows for "insanely fast" inference (tens of thousands of tokens per second), driving the cost of intelligence toward the floor.

System-Level Optimization Levers

  • Extreme Codesign: Carter Abdallah (NVIDIA) argues that infrastructure must be treated as a "system problem." Efficiency cannot be achieved if suppliers only meet for the first time on the data center floor.
  • Infrastructure Innovations:
      • Power Transportation: Transitioning to 800-volt DC systems to minimize "leaks" during energy transport to the rack.
      • Liquid Cooling: Moving to closed-loop systems using 45°C (113°F) water, eliminating the need for energy-intensive air conditioning and water evaporation.
      • Modular Design: NVIDIA is building its own gigawatt factory (DSX) to standardize designs and provide the ecosystem with a "blueprint" for efficient token production.
  • Software Optimization: Tools like NVIDIA NIM and Dynamo enable disaggregated inference and better utilization, ensuring the "factory" isn't sitting idle or "punching one cup out of a giant sheet of metal and throwing the rest away."

The Mechanics of "Hard" World-Building

  • Scientific Derivation: Weir does not arbitrarily choose plot points; he derives them from math. For *Project Hail Mary*, he calculated the exact temperature required for the fictional "Astrophage" to generate neutrinos (96.415°C) based on particle velocity and mass-energy.
  • The "Wrong Math" Spreadsheet: To maintain internal consistency, Weir created a spreadsheet specifically for "wrong math." This calculated how the alien species (the Eridians) would have over-fueled their ships because they lacked a discovery of relativity and relied on Newtonian physics.
  • Special Relativity as a Narrative Limit: The maximum distance for the Astrophage infection (eight light-years) was derived by calculating how long the organism could survive before radiating away its heat energy, adjusted for its own inertial reference frame.

Designing Rocky: Logic-Driven Alien Biology

  • Environmental Constraints: Weir started with the exoplanet 40 Eridani A b. Because the planet is close to its star (a 46-day year), he deduced it needed a thick atmosphere and a massive magnetic field to keep liquid water at 200°C without boiling away.
  • Atmospheric Physics:
      • Composition: He chose an ammonia atmosphere because ammonia is a heavy molecule that is harder for solar winds to strip away from a gravity well.
      • Planetary Shape: Due to its 6-hour rotation and proximity to the Roche limit, Weir calculated the planet would be significantly oblong, resulting in different gravity measurements at the poles versus the equator.
  • Evolutionary Outcomes:
      • Echolocation: Because no light reaches the surface through the thick atmosphere, the aliens evolved echolocation so precise it functions like vision.
      • Inorganic Construction: Eridians are described as being like a "walking beehive," where internal worker cells build a mostly inorganic carapace, resulting in pentagonal symmetry.
      • Internalized Biosphere: Since the atmosphere contains no oxygen, the aliens have an internalized system where they consume food to maintain a biological "internal biosphere" rather than exchanging gases with the environment.

Collaborative Case Study: Detecting Intelligent Life

  • Energy Signatures: When brainstorming a new story with Cleo Abram, Weir suggested looking for "energy absorption." A dead planet reflects/emits all radiation it receives; a living planet stores it in chemical bonds (plants/algae), creating a measurable deficit in radiation.
  • The "Steve’s World" Scenario: In their real-time story collaboration, they imagined an amateur astronomer detecting a sudden electromagnetic burst from a planet (not a star).
      • Technosignatures: This would prompt a search for CFCs or pollution—gases that do not occur via natural processes—indicating an industrial civilization.
  • The "Swords to Plowshares" Greeting: To signal back to a civilization 20 light-years away, Weir suggested detonating a sequence of nuclear bombs in deep space.
      • Visibility: By moving the bombs far from the Sun (e.g., near Saturn or Neptune), the contrast against the dark background of space would make the signal visible to distant telescopes.

The Role of Optimism and Public Feedback

  • Crowdsourced Fact-Checking: *The Martian* was originally published chapter-by-chapter online. Readers flagged errors, such as Weir forgetting that hydrogen and oxygen would mix evenly in the "Hab" rather than separating by weight.
  • Narrative Optimism: Weir avoids dystopias, focusing instead on "good people solving hard problems." He notes that while the news reports on misery, it is only "news" because the norm is actually humans helping one another.
  • The Purpose of Sci-Fi: For Weir, the ultimate goal is not just education but entertainment. He aims for the "that was cool" feeling that inspired him as a child.
