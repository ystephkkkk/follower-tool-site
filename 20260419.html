TL;DR

Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz have reached a critical impasse as Iran maintains its blockade, prompting the Trump administration to deploy special envoy Steve Witkoff to Pakistan for high-stakes negotiations while simultaneously threatening to target Iranian infrastructure.

Middle East Crisis and the Strait of Hormuz

  • Strait of Hormuz Standstill: Shipping remains at a near standstill after Iran reversed its decision to reopen the waterway.
      • Iran has stated the strait will remain closed until the US lifts its blockade on Iranian ports.
      • Multiple ships, including at least nine tankers, have been forced to reverse course; the Indian government expressed deep concern after shooting incidents involving two of its flagged vessels.
  • Escalating US Response: President Trump issued a severe warning via social media, stating that if a deal is not reached, the US will "knock out every single power plant" and bridge in Iran.
      • The *Wall Street Journal* reports the US military is preparing to board Iran-linked tankers and seize commercial ships in international waters.
      • Negotiation Strategy: Special envoy Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner are scheduled to lead a delegation to Islamabad, Pakistan, for talks beginning Tuesday.
  • Internal Iranian Friction: Analysts noted visible fissures within the Iranian regime, as the parliamentary speaker leads negotiations while the IRGC and hardline factions appear to pull back on concessions.

The Federal Reserve Leadership Battle

  • Kevin Warsh Confirmation: Trump’s pick for Federal Reserve Chair faces a Senate Banking Committee hearing this week amidst heavy political friction.
      • Legislative Gridlock: Senator Thom Tillis (R-NC) continues to block all Fed nominations until the administration drops its investigation into outgoing Chair Jerome Powell.
      • Financial Disclosures: Senator Elizabeth Warren (D-MA) has raised concerns regarding Warsh’s financial disclosures, which reveal a personal net worth of approximately $190 million.
  • Powell’s Uncertain Departure: Jerome Powell’s term expires on May 15, but he may remain on the Board of Governors to maintain leverage against the administration's ongoing probes.
      • Trump has publicly threatened to fire Powell if he does not leave "on time," though experts suggest the markets may doubt the president's legal authority to succeed in a forced removal.

Blue Origin’s Commercial Expansion

  • New Glenn Rocket Launch: Blue Origin successfully launched its 98-meter heavy-lift New Glenn rocket from Cape Canaveral, achieving a milestone in booster reuse.
      • The booster, which originally flew in November, successfully landed on a sea-based vessel for the first time.
      • Payload Details: The rocket carried a satellite for AST SpaceMobile, intended to serve as a "cell tower in space" for direct-to-device cellular service.
  • Strategic Pivot to the Moon: CEO Dave Limp confirmed the company has paused its "New Shepard" space tourism program to focus entirely on NASA's Artemis program.
      • The company aims to put a cargo-focused lunar lander on the moon by the end of this year.
      • Blue Origin currently targets a cadence of 8 to 12 flights per year, aiming to compete directly with SpaceX’s Falcon 9 and Starship.

Global Economic and Security Briefs

  • Airline Cancellations: Rising jet fuel prices are forcing global carriers to reduce capacity for May; data from Cirium shows 19 of the 20 largest airlines are cutting scheduled flights.
  • Cuban Energy Crisis: Havana is facing severe blackouts as the US oil embargo deepens and shipments from Venezuela and Mexico cease. The Trump administration reportedly sent a delegation to Cuba last week to demand economic and political changes within a "narrow window."
  • Sustainable "Blue Foods": Chef Andrew Zimmern advocated for a shift toward "blue foods" (water-based proteins), noting that by 2045, the US may produce more seafood by weight than potatoes.
