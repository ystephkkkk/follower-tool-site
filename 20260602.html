TL;DR

At Snowflake Summit 2026, CEO Sridhar Ramaswamy declared the arrival of the "Agentic Enterprise," introducing Snowflake Intelligence and Cortex Code (CoCo) as secure, governance-first control planes designed to coordinate data, models, and SaaS applications to automate end-to-end business workflows.

The Four Pillars of the Agentic Enterprise

To transition from isolated AI pilots to a fully autonomous "Agentic Enterprise," organizations must coordinate four foundational components through a unified system of decision.

  • Enterprise Context: Proprietary data (structured and unstructured) acts as a company's ultimate competitive moat, serving as the foundational context that makes AI outputs relevant and strategically valuable.
  • AI Models: Organizations require the flexibility to deploy a mix of frontier and open-source models (including Anthropic’s Claude, Mistral, Gemini, and OpenAI’s GPT) optimized for specific speed, reasoning, or cost demands.
  • SaaS & Application Integration: AI must interact directly with the daily systems of record that run the business, such as SAP, Salesforce, Zoom, and Microsoft 365.
  • The Agentic Control Plane: Snowflake introduced two major solutions to act as a secure, governed mission control:
      • Snowflake Intelligence: A personal assistant for business users that queries enterprise data and triggers actions across SaaS applications using natural language.
      • Cortex Code ("CoCo"): An AI-powered developer agent that automates database migrations, builds data pipelines, monitors query costs, and writes code using natural language.
      • Notoma Acquisition: Snowflake announced its intent to acquire Notoma to bring Anthropic's Model Context Protocol (MCP) natively into the AI Data Cloud. This secures and governs AI interactions across external platforms (e.g., Slack, GitHub, Google Drive) with human-in-the-loop approvals.

Realizing P&L Outcomes: Accenture's Strategy

Accenture leadership outlined how the global services giant is executing its own internal agentic transformation while steering thousands of clients toward tangible business outcomes.

  • Workforce Restructuring: Accenture reorganized roles for all 750,000 of its global employees around seven C-suite personas, establishing a standardized Snowflake-backed data foundation for each.
  • Overcoming Data Debt: Accenture estimates that 85% of enterprise clients are currently held back by severely fragmented data and legacy process debt.
  • Focus on the P&L: Leadership emphasizes that AI projects must directly tie to a line item on the P&L—either driving revenue or reducing Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and SG&A.
  • Quantifiable Success Metrics: In a joint engagement for a major European utility company, Accenture and Snowflake delivered:
      • Project execution cycles reduced from months to 12 weeks.
      • Analytical query times dropped from weeks to two seconds.
      • Compute resource times reduced by 85%.

Custom Workflows on the Data Lake: Sanofi Case Study

Pharmaceutical giant Sanofi explained how it shifted from passive business intelligence (dashboards) to active agentic workflows to support operations across R&D, manufacturing, and sales.

  • Bypassing SaaS Silos: Rather than building brittle pipelines to pull data out of expensive SaaS applications, Sanofi designs deterministic agentic workflows directly on top of its Snowflake data lake.
  • Targeted Value Areas: Sanofi is deploying these custom agentic workflows to optimize its €18 billion annual procurement spend, automate IT and HR support, and empower its field sales teams.
  • Live "Concierge" Demo: Sanofi demonstrated its real-time, voice-activated "Concierge" agent built for field sales representatives:
      • The agent pulled doctor metrics directly from live production systems, detailed historical patient treatment trends, suggested conversational icebreakers, and drafted and sent a post-meeting summary email instantly.

The Future of Models, Scaling Laws, and Trust

Daniela Amodei, President and Co-founder of Anthropic, joined Sridhar Ramaswamy to discuss the exponential trajectory of AI capabilities and how enterprises should prepare.

  • Designing for Future Scaling Laws: Because AI models improve predictably and rapidly with more compute and data, Amodei advises businesses not to build solely for today’s capabilities, but to "dream big" and design architectures capable of absorbing rapid model advancements.
  • Trust as an Accelerant: In the enterprise space, safety, predictability, and low hallucination rates are not obstacles to speed—they are prerequisites. Organizations can only scale AI once they have built absolute trust in the safety of the output.
  • The Power of Precision: Snowflake's AI strategy prioritizes precision over recall (e.g., in text-to-SQL conversions with Cortex Analyst), ensuring the model safely declines to answer a query rather than returning a hallucinated or incorrect data point.
  • Real-World Deployment: Payments firm Block (formerly Square) is combining Snowflake's data platform with Claude Code to run real-time fraud analysis and payment processing insights at massive scale.
