TL;DR: Spencer Pratt is running for Mayor of Los Angeles on a platform of "radical accountability," proposing to end the city's homelessness and wildfire crises by enforcing existing laws, auditing the $25B "NGO industrial complex," and replacing career bureaucrats with $1-a-year private sector executives.

The Catalyst: The Palisades Fire and Institutional Failure

  • Personal Loss as a Policy Driver: Pratt’s decision to run was triggered by the loss of his home in the January 2024 Palisades fire, which he attributes to gross city negligence.
      • Resource Mismanagement: Claims the city drained the 5-million-gallon Palisades reservoir in June 2024 without a backup plan, leaving firefighters without local water assets.
      • Leadership Void: Alleged that Mayor Karen Bass was in Africa during the crisis and her Deputy Mayor was on house arrest, resulting in a failure to request fixed-wing air support to drop water.
      • Lack of Communication: Noted a total absence of emergency sirens or door-to-door evacuations, forcing residents to track the fire via security cameras and social media.

The "NGO Industrial Complex" and Homelessness

  • Audit-First Approach: Pratt intends to bring in the IRS criminal investigation team during his first week to audit every NGO receiving city funds.
  • The NGO Loophole: Claims massive corruption where organizations like "FireAid" raise $100M with little to no direct distribution to victims.
      • Real Estate Inflations: Alleges the city buys buildings for $29M that were listed for $11M days prior, with "kickbacks" going to developers while the buildings remain empty.
      • Housing Costs: Cites costs of $700,000 per person for "prison-like" housing that lacks mandatory drug treatment requirements.
  • Mandatory Treatment: Proposes moving the homeless population out of residential neighborhoods and into nature-based compounds with specialized services for veterans, families, and addicts.
      • Enforcement: Plans to post city-wide signage giving a 2-3 week warning before strictly enforcing laws against public drug use, nudity, and encampments.

Economic Revival and "Auto-Greenlighting" Business

  • AI-Driven Permitting: Aims to replace the "permitting nightmare" with AI systems to provide instant zoning and permit approvals if criteria are met, eliminating the current years-long wait times for small businesses.
  • Independent Hollywood: Focuses on reviving the entertainment industry by prioritizing independent filmmakers and artists.
      • Fee Elimination: Plans to remove filming fees for indie productions and ensure street safety so crews don't have to "pay off gangs" to film on location.
  • Private-Sector Partnerships: Claims to have secured interest from billionaires willing to fund infrastructure and a "Fun Czar" willing to donate $500M to revitalize city culture.

Governance and Operational Strategy

  • The "Cincinnatus" Model: Pratt views his run as a "tour of duty" (8 years) rather than a career, intending to return to private life once the city is stabilized.
  • Recruiting Elite Talent: Plans to bypass traditional political appointments by hiring CEOs who have managed $50B budgets to run city departments for $1 a year.
  • Fiscal Transparency: Proposes a "CliffNotes-level" digital dashboard where every dollar of taxpayer money can be tracked by the public in real-time.
  • Public Safety Priority: Argues that while union leadership backs the incumbent, the rank-and-file LAPD and LAFD support his platform because they want the authority to enforce the law without fear of political retaliation.
