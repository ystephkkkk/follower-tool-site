TL;DR

Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth and General Dan Caine reported that "Operation Epic Fury" has struck over 11,000 Iranian targets in 30 days, successfully degrading Iran’s naval and missile capabilities while maintaining military pressure to force a diplomatic deal regarding nuclear disarmament.

Operational Status: "Operation Epic Fury"

  • Targeting Success: The U.S. joint force has struck more than 11,000 targets in the first month of the conflict.
      • Dynamic Strikes: In a single 24-hour period, U.S. pilots executed 200 "dynamic strikes"—real-time targeting of mobile assets like launchers and troop formations based on fresh intelligence.
      • Nuclear Deterrence: Operations are specifically targeting nuclear weapons research labs and infrastructure to ensure Iran never achieves a weapons-grade (90%) enrichment level.
  • Aviation Milestones: General Caine confirmed the first overland B-52 missions, signaling U.S. air superiority over Iranian territory.
  • Naval Dominance: The U.S. has destroyed more than 150 Iranian ships, including the entire fleet of Jamaran-class frigates.
      • Efforts are now shifting toward neutralizing Iran's minelaying capabilities and naval supply depots using attack helicopters and close air support.

Diplomatic Strategy and "The Deal"

  • Negotiation Status: Secretary Hegseth confirmed that active negotiations are ongoing involving key administration figures.
      • "Negotiating with Bombs": Hegseth stated that while a diplomatic solution is the primary goal, military intensity will increase until Iran relinquishes its nuclear ambitions.
  • Strait of Hormuz: Reopening the vital waterway remains a core objective, though Hegseth challenged international allies to take a larger role.
      • Allied Responsibility: He specifically called on the UK’s Royal Navy and other nations to assist, noting that the U.S. is less dependent on this oil route than its allies.
  • Regime Change: Hegseth alluded to a "new regime" currently in place in Iran, suggesting they must be "wiser than the last" to avoid total industrial destruction.

Economic and Industrial Impact

  • Domestic Energy Costs: Gas prices have surpassed a national average of $4.02 per gallon, a psychological benchmark not seen since 2022.
      • Crude oil benchmarks saw a 55% increase in March—the highest one-month jump since 1988.
  • The "Arsenal of Freedom": General Caine praised the U.S. defense industrial base, emphasizing that the "distance from the factory floor to the front line is incredibly short."
      • Hegseth noted that while Iran’s industrial base is nearly destroyed and unable to reconstitute, the U.S. is rebuilding its own at "wartime speed."

Ground Force Posture and Morale

  • "Boots on the Ground": Hegseth refused to rule out a ground invasion, stating that "unpredictability" is essential for victory.
      • He emphasized that the administration will not repeat the mistakes of Iraq or Afghanistan by setting public timelines or foreclosing military options.
  • Troop Sentiment: Following a visit to CENTCOM, Hegseth reported high morale and an "urgency to finish the job."
      • Defensive Readiness: U.S. forces are utilizing layered defenses (Patriots, THAAD, electronic warfare) and "dispersal" tactics to protect assets from Iranian missile barrages, which have recently decreased in frequency.

  • Domestic Production: General Caine highlighted the "national assembly line" as the core of American combat power, praising machinists and technicians for maintaining the precision required for high-tech warfare.
