The Evolution from Palantir to Helsing

  • Background & Expertise: Robert Fink holds a PhD from Oxford in Database Theory and spent nearly eight years at Palantir, where he led the engineering team that built Foundry from scratch.
  • The Transition: Fink joined Helsing in 2021 as CTO after a series of meetings with co-founder Niklas Köhler. He describes his recruitment as an "asymmetric date" where he provided unsolicited advice before being asked to build the tech team from zero.
  • The "Software-First" Mission:
      • European Sovereignty: Fink argues that Europe will be worse off if it does not develop its own high-end AI defense capabilities, moving away from total reliance on U.S. providers.
      • Disrupting the "Primes": Helsing aims to operate on equal footing with established giants like Airbus, Saab, and Thales by prioritizing AI and software over traditional hardware-centric development.

Product Ecosystem: Drones, Jets, and Electronic Warfare

  • Electronic Warfare (Eurofighter): Helsing’s first major success was a collaboration with Saab to upgrade the German Eurofighter fleet. Helsing provides the AI-driven radar software that helps pilots identify and classify threats (e.g., surface-to-air missiles) in real-time.
  • The HX2 Drone:
      • Scale & Cost: Recently approved by the German parliament in a €274 million contract.
      • Innovation: Features GPS-independent navigation, allowing the drone to operate in environments where Russian or other adversarial jamming would normally disable standard systems.
  • CA1 (Unmanned Combat Aircraft):
      • The "Loyal Wingman": A 4-5 ton unmanned jet currently in development.
      • Cost Efficiency: Targeted to cost roughly 10% of a manned fighter jet (approx. €10–20 million vs. €100+ million).
      • Timeline: First flight is scheduled for 2025, with full operational capability targeted for 2029/2030.
  • Space & Reconnaissance: Helsing is expanding into satellite-based "Synthetic Aperture Radar" (SAR) to provide autonomous reconnaissance and communication systems, effectively building a European sovereign alternative to portions of Starlink.

AI and the Future of Warfare

  • The End of Human Pilots: Fink asserts that in air-to-air combat, AI dominance is already a "done deal." Human pilots will soon have no chance against AI-driven systems in dogfights.
  • The Role of the Human:
      • Operator in the Loop: Despite the autonomy of the systems, Fink emphasizes that for ethical and tactical reasons, a human must remain the final decision-maker ("Ja/Nein" authority) on the use of force.
      • Physicists’ Approach: Fink uses his physics background to solve complex navigation and flight dynamic problems that were previously too expensive to solve on low-cost hardware.
  • Manufacturing Revolution:
      • Attrition Warfare: In the Ukraine conflict, drones are used by the millions. Fink emphasizes that hardware must be designed for mass-producibility like ammunition, rather than as artisan-crafted, complex machinery.

Organizational Culture and Strategy

  • The 10x Effect: Fink believes AI does not replace top talent but acts as a multiplier. He argues that a top-tier engineer equipped with AI is "10 times more effective," making the hunt for "the best of the best" even more critical than in the past.
  • The "Defense Tech" Shift: Since the invasion of Ukraine, Fink has observed a massive cultural shift in Germany. Top talent from Fintech and "Lifestyle" startups are now actively seeking roles in defense to protect European democratic values.
  • Investment & Growth: Backed by Daniel Ek (Spotify founder) with an initial €100 million investment, Helsing is focused on pre-investing in product development—a departure from the traditional defense model of waiting for government contracts before starting work.
