Executive Briefing: NVIDIA & Eli Lilly: The AI Revolution in Drug Discovery

TL;DR: NVIDIA and Eli Lilly are partnering to revolutionize drug discovery by building the world's largest dedicated on-prem biology supercomputer and a co-innovation lab, applying advanced AI to simulate human biology and accelerate the development of new medicines.

## The NVIDIA AI Advantage & Accelerated Computing

  • Accelerated Computing Evolution:
      • NVIDIA has pioneered accelerated computing for 33 years, achieving 1 millionx AI acceleration in 10 years, significantly outpacing Moore's Law (100x over 10 years).
      • This acceleration, achieved by co-designing the entire computing stack, aims to tackle humanity's most impactful challenges.
  • Transformative AI Breakthroughs:
      • Computer Graphics: Ray tracing, once a decades-long problem, is now real-time and photorealistic in games, augmented by AI.
      • Self-Driving Cars: Approaching a "ChatGPT moment," with robo-taxi capabilities becoming standard in new cars within a decade, driven by multimodality AI and reasoning.
      • Generative AI & LLMs: Recent breakthroughs in multimodality AI, reasoning, and agentic capabilities have transformed AI into an "incredibly useful" tool, applicable across diverse industries, including life sciences.
  • NVIDIA's Life Sciences Platforms:
      • Key Libraries: NVIDIA offers platforms like Parabricks for gene sequencing (achieving a Guinness World Record), MONAI (an open-source platform for medical imaging with 6 million downloads), and BioNeMo for understanding geometric structures like molecules and proteins.
      • Dominant Role: The vast majority of the world's structure prediction models are built on NVIDIA platforms today.
      • Pre-trained Models: BioNeMo includes pre-trained models and data for protein design (Laortina), molecular synthesis (Resin), toxicity prediction (Kermit), DNA foundation models (Evo 2), and RNA design (Codon FM).
      • Future Vision: Developing foundation models for proteins, with foundation models for cells "just around the corner."

## Eli Lilly's Innovation Strategy & GLP-1 Success

  • Resilience & Accelerated R&D:
      • Eli Lilly, a 150-year-old company, has a history of navigating challenging industry cycles and patent cliffs by being "relentless on invention," uniquely surviving without mergers.
      • Streamlined Processes: Lilly systematically optimized its R&D, making it approximately 40% faster than other large pharmaceutical companies, enabling invention within existing patent cycles.
  • GLP-1 Market Leadership:
      • Pioneering Role: Lilly launched the world's first GLP-1 treatment in 2006.
      • Strategic Execution: Through sustained innovation, including the development of Tirzepatide (a dual GLP-1/GIP medicine like Zepbound), Lilly became the market leader in GLP-1 treatments last year, with a triple-acting variant anticipated in 2027.
  • Continuous Innovation: CEO David Ricks emphasizes the ongoing need to create conditions for new "success cycles" to ensure sustained growth and impact beyond current blockbuster drugs.

## A Transformative Partnership: NVIDIA & Eli Lilly Co-Innovate

  • Shared Vision for Infrastructure: Both Jensen Huang and David Ricks highlight the critical importance of building advanced computing infrastructure to attract top scientific talent and foster groundbreaking discoveries in AI and biology.
  • Key Partnership Pillars:
      • Biology Supercomputer: The world's largest dedicated on-prem biology supercomputer is being built in Indianapolis, expected to be operational this week.
      • Bay Area Co-Innovation Lab: A joint Lilly-NVIDIA AI lab will be established in the Silicon Valley area, bringing together leading computer scientists and drug discovery experts.
  • AI-Driven Discovery Mechanisms:
      • Foundation Models: The lab will focus on developing new foundation models for proteins and cells.
      • Closed-Loop Scientific Flywheel: AI will drive protein/chemical synthesis, which will then be validated in robotic wet labs to collect ground truth data. This data will continuously feed back into the models, creating a "scientific flywheel on steroids" to accelerate discovery.
  • "Digital Twin" for Biology:
      • Jensen Huang envisions applying the "computer-aided design" paradigm, which allows 15,000 engineers to design complex chips that work perfectly on the first try, to human biology.
      • This aims to transform drug discovery from an "empirical" process (like "wandering around the forest looking for truffles") into a precise engineering problem, performing much of the work "in silico."

## Beyond Obesity: AI's Role in Future Healthcare Frontiers

  • Broadening GLP-1 Impact:
      • Chronic Disease Lynchpin: Obesity is a key driver for over 200 chronic diseases; GLP-1s provide a synthetic "off switch" for hunger, addressing modern caloric abundance.
      • Expanded Benefits: Zepbound users experience an average 23% body weight loss and a 93% reduction in pre-diabetes conversion. Emerging research shows non-obvious benefits, including reduced chronic inflammation (e.g., improved psoriatic arthritis) and potential in brain health (addiction, gambling, alcohol, smoking cessation).
      • Future Delivery & Reach: An oral GLP-1 form is set to launch this spring, significantly enhancing convenience and global scalability, especially for regions with high diabetes prevalence like Southeast Asia.
      • Historical Parallel: David Ricks likens the potential impact of GLP-1s to that of antibiotics, which drove a 60-year wave of medical breakthroughs by conquering acute infections.
  • Focus on Healthy Longevity: Eli Lilly's approach to longevity is to extend healthy human lifespan by eradicating disease (e.g., through GLP-1s addressing chronic ailments) rather than solely focusing on extending maximum biological shelf-life.
  • Next Frontier: Brain Health: Significant efforts are directed at tackling dementia and other diseases of the aging brain, leveraging AI to simulate brain tissue, understand complex interactions (e.g., inflammation, protein misfolding), and discover new therapeutic targets.

## Accelerating Drug Discovery Through AI & Collaboration

  • AI Lab Research Directives:
      • Drug Engineering/Optimization: A highly tractable problem for the joint lab, focusing on optimizing new modalities (e.g., RNA, gene therapies) and refining drug designs for known targets.
      • Target Discovery: A more challenging endeavor requiring robotic wet labs to run 24/7, generating massive experimental data to fully profile targets and their structural complexities, closing the loop with engineered drug designs.
  • Synthetic Data Flywheel: The ultimate vision involves creating a "synthetic data flywheel" where AI models are trained and tested against sophisticated "world models" (representing the lab itself), reducing the need for constant physical experimentation while remaining grounded by real-world data.
  • Collaborative Ecosystem:
      • Tune Labs: Lilly's visionary platform, built on NVIDIA's federated learning (NVFlare), enables startups and third parties to collaborate on drug discovery, contributing data and benefiting from improved models without co-mingling sensitive intellectual property.
      • Broader AI in Healthcare: AI is rapidly being adopted in healthcare services (e.g., agentic AI, Lilly Direct's $4B/year pharmacy platform) to enhance efficiency and foster innovation, despite existing industry frictions.
  • Lilly's Tech Legacy: David Ricks highlights Lilly's long history of leveraging cutting-edge technology, including developing the first approved biotech product (Humulin with Genentech), acquiring the first pharmaceutical supercomputer (a Cray "Big Red"), and designing the first computer-designed medicine (Humalog). This partnership is a natural evolution of that legacy.
