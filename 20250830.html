TL;DR

Kimi founder Yang Zhilin reflects on two years of AI entrepreneurship following the release of Kimi K2, noting that while the industry faces persistent "data walls" and evaluation challenges, the pursuit of scaling through test-time compute, Muon optimizers, and generalist Agentic systems continues to drive the relentless climb toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).

1. Two-Year Retrospective & The "Endless Mountain" Metaphor

  • Evolving Landscape: Reflecting on the past two years of AI development, Yang likens the journey to climbing an endless snow-capped mountain (*The Beginning of Infinity* framework).
    • Core Insight: Problems are inevitable, but problems are solvable; solving one technological hurdle immediately gives rise to a new set of challenges (e.g., transitioning from RL training hurdles to complex end-to-end evaluation and verification).
    • AI as an Amplifier: Kimi now leverages the K2 model itself to handle data processing, model analysis, and internal training tasks, effectively using AI as an accelerator to climb the mountain.
  • Redefining AGI: AGI is no longer viewed as a single, sudden step-function milestone (like landing on the moon), but rather as an ongoing direction of travel where models outperform 99% of humans across specific domains while society gradually digests the long-cycle economic and operational impacts.

2. Technical Breakthroughs: K1.5, K2, and the Muon Optimizer

  • From Pre-training/SFT to RL: The 2024–2025 shift centered on moving away from heavy reliance on pre-training and SFT toward pre-training combined with reinforcement learning (RL) and multi-turn agentic behaviors.
  • K1.5 Validation: Validated end-to-end RL training without heavy reliance on process reward models or explicit value functions, successfully building out internal RL infrastructure and algorithmic know-how.
  • K2 & Token Efficiency: Confronting the global "data wall" (the slow growth of high-quality textual data), K2 prioritizes Token Efficiency over sheer computational speed to ensure models extract maximum intelligence per token.
    • Muon Optimizer: Departing from the decade-old Adam optimizer, Kimi adopted the Muon optimizer (originally proposed by Keller and scaled up for K2 after overcoming max logit explosion hurdles).
    • Efficiency Gain: Under compute-optimal conditions, Muon delivers roughly a 2x improvement in Token Efficiency—meaning training on 30T high-quality tokens yields the intelligence equivalent of 60T tokens under Adam.

3. Agentic Systems and Generalization

  • Defining Agents: True agents represent a transition from a "brain in a vat" (isolated reasoning) to active, multi-turn interactions with the external world utilizing diverse tools (browsers, search engines, and code execution).
  • Current Bottlenecks: Generalization remains the primary hurdle for agents; models risk overfitting to specific benchmarks (like SWE-bench) or predefined human tasks, failing in Out-Of-Distribution (OOD) scenarios.
  • The Coding Sub-cluster: While Coding Agents represent a critical subset of agentic capability because code serves as the primary tool for digital automation and context management, the long-term goal remains a fully generalist model rather than a narrow coding assistant.
  • Long Context Challenges: Effective agentic tasks require context windows scaling to millions of tokens, but architectural trade-offs (such as linear attention biases) risk degrading the model's overall reasoning IQ.

4. Ecosystem Views: Open Source vs. Closed Source

  • Strategic Openness: While foundational base models heavily favor closed or proprietary approaches due to infrastructure and talent concentration, Moonshot AI chooses to periodically open-source key technologies and technical know-how (such as K2-based architectures) to accelerate community progress ("Race to the Top").
  • Market Consolidation: Yang predicts that the global AI market will inevitably consolidate, shrinking from hundreds of players down to a stable few market leaders.

5. Organization, Product, and Management Philosophy

  • Model-as-a-Product: The traditional app-store user acquisition playbook matters less in the AI era; building an elite product is tightly coupled with training the underlying model (the system design requires building the environment and toolchains concurrently with the model).
  • RL-Driven Management: Drawing parallels between scientific knowledge creation and team leadership, Yang applies an "RL mindset" to managing company research—balancing top-down priors (SFT) to prevent teams from drifting off-course with bottom-up rewards (RL) to encourage autonomy and combat reward hacking.

Predictions & Forward Calls

  • Model Scaling: Yang Zhilin predicts that compute scaling (FLOPs scaling) via pre-training and RL will continue to dominate and outpace data-driven feedback loops in the near term because neural networks remain hyper-sensitive to noise in user-feedback data.
  • Market Consolidation: Yang Zhilin predicts the global foundation model market will compress from dozens of players down to a stable group of just "a few" major industry survivors.
