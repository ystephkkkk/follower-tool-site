TL;DR

Anthropic's release of Fable 5 has ignited developer outrage over aggressive censorship, secret performance downgrades, and 30-day data retention policies, highlighting a broader shift toward open-source models as tech firms lobby for restrictive AI regulations. At the same time, macro concerns mount with hot CPI and PPI inflation prints, alongside fierce debates over wealth redistribution proposals and the integrity of California's election laws.

Anthropic's Fable 5 Backlash and the Open-Source Shift

  • Model Capabilities & High Costs: Anthropic released its "Mythos-level" Fable 5 model, which tops nearly every performance benchmark but costs twice as much per token as Opus 4.8.
  • Surveillance & Data Retention Policies:
      • 30-Day Storage: Anthropic now retains all prompt and output data for 30 days, offering no exceptions even for enterprise customers with pre-existing zero-data-retention agreements.
      • Secret Performance "Nerfing": Developers discovered that Anthropic was secretly downgrading user access to lesser models if its system detected competitive AI research, chip design, or sensitive scientific prompts.
      • Over-Censorship: Users reported being downgraded or blocked for innocuous queries, such as questions about mitochondria or Ben Thompson's (Stratechery) query on GLP-1 cancer risks.
  • The Pivot to Open Source:
      • Scientific Restrictions: David Friedberg noted that his company, Ohalo, is losing the ability to use closed models for genomic and RNA guide design due to sweeping "bioweapon" filters.
      • Foreign Model Vulnerability: Because American closed models are increasingly restricted, companies are shifting to open-source models, where Chinese models (like Qwen) currently outpace American open-source options, threatening U.S. competitiveness.
      • Local Solutions: Promising community-funded open-source options like Arc Institute's *Evo 2* genome language model are successfully bypassing closed ecosystems to support research.

AI Regulation, Fearmongering, and "Regulatory Capture"

  • Strategic Capture Play: David Sacks argued that Anthropic is leading a highly sophisticated regulatory capture campaign. By fearmongering about existential risk, they hope to lobby the government into banning or heavily regulating open-source alternatives that they cannot compete with in a free market.
  • Calls for FDA-Style Oversight: Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei released a 5,000-word blog post arguing that transparency is no longer enough and calling for an FAA- or FDA-style regulatory agency to pre-approve all frontier models.
  • Hypocrisy in the Safety Narrative: Critics pointed out the hypocrisy of Anthropic's public warnings. Despite publishing papers warning that "recursive self-improvement" could end the world, the firm recently hired AI pioneer Andrej Karpathy specifically to run recursive self-improvement.
  • The Safe Alternative: Sacks noted that instead of restricting model inputs/access, guardrails should be placed downstream at physical bottlenecks (e.g., codifying mandatory synthetic DNA screening for labs ordering raw physical materials).

Bernie Sanders' 50% AI Tax vs. Sovereign Wealth Reform

  • The Sanders Proposal: Senator Bernie Sanders introduced the *American AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act*, proposing a one-time 50% stock tax on the largest AI firms (including OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI) to seed a public sovereign wealth fund with voting rights.
  • The "Stupidity Tax" on AI CEOs: Sacks remarked that while he opposes property confiscation, AI CEOs have brought this on themselves. By repeatedly telling the public that AI will cause "50% job loss," executives have practically invited politicians to seize their equity to fund a social safety net.
  • Reforming Social Security: Friedberg proposed a counter-alternative: convert the bankrupt U.S. Social Security Trust Fund (which currently only holds U.S. Treasuries) into an active Sovereign Wealth Fund. By investing in equities and top-tier American tech companies, citizens would directly share in the wealth generated by technological productivity.
  • High Marginal Costs of AI: Chamath Palihapitiya highlighted a critical structural difference between the internet and AI:
      • The internet had a marginal cost of zero for new users, generating infinite profit margins.
      • AI has incredibly high marginal costs, taxing physical GPUs, electricity, and memory for every single query. Therefore, AI cannot easily absorb heavy taxation or equity seizures without crippling its operational viability.

Rising Inflation and the Macroeconomic Reality

  • CPI & PPI Hot Prints: May inflation numbers came in significantly higher than expected:
      • CPI (Consumer Price Index): Rose to 4.2% year-over-year, marking its highest level since April 2023.
      • PPI (Producer Price Index): Jumped to 6.5% year-over-year, its highest level since the end of 2022.
  • Rising Rate Outlook: Driven by aggressive government deficit spending and energy shocks related to geopolitical instability, Friedberg warned that the market could see Federal Reserve overnight rates reach 5.5% to 6.0%.
  • The Chinese Oil Wildcard: Palihapitiya warned that China has been smoothing global energy consumption. If China runs out of its strategic reserves and is forced back into the spot market to buy an extra 3 million barrels a day, global oil prices could easily spike to $150–$200 a barrel, sending CPI into a tailspin.

California's Election Laws and Integrity Concerns

  • The Mayoral Primary Discrepancy: The group debated the highly anomalous results of the Los Angeles local elections, where candidate Spencer Pratt led by a wide margin in-person, only for late-arriving mail-in ballots to swing a statistically improbable 80% increase to opponent Nithya Raman.
  • Systemic Vulnerabilities: Friedberg and Sacks argued that California's legislative environment has turned elections into "appointments" via loopholes:
      • AB 1921: Legalized unlimited, third-party ballot harvesting.
      • Universal Mail-in Ballots: Sent to millions of inactive or outdated addresses on dirty voter rolls, leading to piles of loose, unmonitored ballots in apartment complexes.
      • Lax Verification: No voter ID requirements, weak signature-matching algorithms, and the ability to register using non-standard identification such as gym memberships.
  • An Impending Crisis of Faith: Friedberg warned that regardless of political affiliation, removing simple voter ID requirements destroys public faith in the democratic process. He called on Congress to establish clear federal standards of one registered citizen, one vote, with strict ID verification.

The AI Landscape and Khosla’s Contrarian Investment Philosophy

  • The OpenAI Gamble: In early 2019, Khosla Ventures committed a $50 million check to OpenAI—double the size of any initial check Vinod Khosla had written in his 40-year career.

      • The Core Thesis: Khosla focused entirely on the massive, asymmetric upside of AI. He argues that trying to forecast exact returns via spreadsheets is misleading for highly disruptive, non-linear technologies.
  • Geographic Concentration: Silicon Valley remains the absolute epicenter of AI innovation.
      • The Unicorn Divide: SF Bay Area accounts for 91% of all AI unicorns globally. New York is a distant second at just 2%.
  • Scaling and Token Dynamics: Khosla predicts exponential growth in token volume (quantity of inference) alongside rapidly declining costs per token. While "intelligence per token" (quality) will rise more modestly, progress in AI memory and continuous learning will become routine engineering milestones.

Transforming Healthcare, Education, and the Power of "Agency"

  • The End of Human Doctors: Khosla predicts that within five years, human doctors will only be required for interventional medicine (such as surgery or setting broken bones).
      • Triage Accuracy: Standard ChatGPT has a 20% to 30% triage error rate (similar to human GP error rates). However, by layering domain-specific guardrails over models like GPT-5, the triage error rate drops to nearly zero.
      • India Non-Profit Initiative: Khosla is actively working on a non-profit project to deliver AI-driven healthcare across India within the next two years.
  • "Founder Mode" on Personal Challenges: Khosla highlighted GitLab's founder, who upon receiving a terminal cancer diagnosis, went "Founder Mode" on his illness. Using AI to research, design, and run custom vaccines and drugs, he successfully treated his own cancer despite having no medical degree.
  • Fostering "Agency" Over Rote Education:
      • The Real Value of College: Universities must pivot away from lecturing from obsolete textbooks. Khosla advises institutions like IIT Delhi to scrap traditional lecture halls and open more dorms and meeting rooms to foster debate, curiosity, and "agency"—the proactive mindset to solve problems rather than complain about them.

The Robotics Revolution and Severe Economic Deflation

  • The "ChatGPT Moment" for Robotics: Khosla expects a major physical AI breakthrough in the next 2 to 5 years, leading to highly functional humanoid robots capable of doing household tasks like cooking.
      • Automotive-Scale Manufacturing: Humanoid robots will become the dominant physical form factor. They will be built in automotive-style automated factories, driving acquisition costs down dramatically to the raw cost of materials.
  • A Highly Deflationary 2040: By 2040, the baseline cost of living will plummet. Core services like healthcare, education, legal representation, and entertainment will become virtually free.
  • A Warning to India’s IT and BPO Sector: India’s massive IT services and offshore business process outsourcing (BPO) industries "will be gone" due to AI automation. To survive, India must pivot toward becoming the global hub for deploying and integrating AI systems.

Overcoming the AI Energy Bottleneck: Fusion and Geothermal

  • A Five-Year Power Crunch: AI's massive data center power consumption was not forecasted by utility planners. Consequently, energy will bottleneck AI scaling for the next five years. However, this is a short-term issue that will be fully resolved by the early 2030s as new capacity comes online.
  • The 2018 Fusion Bet: Khosla Ventures invested in Bob Momgaard’s MIT spin-out (Commonwealth Fusion Systems) in 2018.
      • Superconductors as the Catalyst: The bet was unlocked by high-temperature superconductors, which allowed for 20 Tesla magnets. This technology effectively shrank the required experimental reactor size to 1/50th of previous designs, accelerating research cycles.
      • The Fusion Timeline: Khosla is highly confident that within two years, the scientific community will agree that fusion works, and within five years, they will agree it is economically viable.
  • Fission vs. Fusion: Khosla views nuclear fission as politically unfeasible in the West due to endless local lawsuits. Once fusion or super-hot geothermal energy (tapping heat above 400°C) is commercialized, the need for fission disappears.
