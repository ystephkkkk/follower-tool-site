TL;DR

Engram is a neolab founded by Dan Biderman and Jessy Lin that is shifting AI memory away from bloated context windows and RAG toward "always training" model weights. By baking a team’s private context directly into white-box model weights, Engram enables personalized, continuously learning models that can match frontier performance while using up to 100x fewer tokens.

The Core Premise: Memory and "Always Training" Weights

  • Continual Learning over Context Engineering: Rather than relying on massive context windows, complex prompting, or external databases, Engram focuses on "continual learning"—constantly training new, evolving contexts directly into model weights.
      • *The Employee Analogy:* The goal is for a model to deeply understand a company's internal priorities, culture, and workflows the way a human employee of several years does, rather than reading files from scratch at test time.
  • Targeting the True AI Bottleneck: Co-founder Jessy Lin argues that the primary bottleneck for AI utility today is not raw intelligence, but rather a model's ability to digest, adapt to, and act on bespoke, evolving organizational context.
  • Workspace-Level Training: Engram is focusing its initial efforts on training per-team models within collaborative workspaces. They are currently partnering with platforms like Microsoft, Notion, and Harvey to capture deep, multi-turn team interactions.

Technical Implementation & the 100x Efficiency Payoff

  • Adapter-Based Architecture: Engram performs fine-tuning on open-source or white-box transformer models using a variety of lightweight techniques, including Low-Rank Adaptations (LoRAs), prefix-tuning, and sparse architectures.
  • Dramatic Token Reduction: By training contextual knowledge directly into the weights, users avoid sending massive files and lengthy system prompts during inference.
      • *The 100x Metric:* A fine-tuned model can answer in 100 tokens what a frontier model would require 100,000 tokens of context to process, driving up to a 100x reduction in token consumption.
  • Bypassing the KV Cache Bottleneck:
      • *The Bottleneck:* Keeping massive contexts in a key-value (KV) cache is highly memory-inefficient. For a 70B parameter Llama model, storing a KV cache for a single long article can consume up to 80 GB of High Bandwidth Memory (HBM)—nearly as much as the entire 100 GB model itself.
      • *The Solution:* Engram uses offline compute to compress this contextual data into model weights via gradient descent, making the representation up to 1,000x smaller and significantly faster to load than a standard KV cache.

Internalization vs. Externalization (The "RAG Killer")

  • A Hybrid Future: While the founders describe their approach as a "RAG killer" for repetitive tasks, they view internalized memory (weights) and externalized memory (RAG) as complementary tools.
      • *Internalized (Weights):* Ideal for implicit knowledge, organizational priorities, bespoke command-line tools, and brand styles.
      • *Externalized (RAG/Search):* Ideal for highly volatile facts that change constantly and do not require conceptual understanding (e.g., a temporary room number).
  • Unlocking Associative Intuition: A model relying solely on RAG cannot make proactive, abstract connections. Weight-based training allows the model to form conceptual associations and surface relevant insights across a team's history without being explicitly prompted to search.

A Contrarian Vision: A Model for Everyone

  • Divergence from Frontier Labs: While frontier labs (like OpenAI or Anthropic) dedicate their primary resources (P0) to building a single, massive, AGI-capable model, Engram envisions a decentralized world where every team and individual runs their own privately trained, specialized model.
  • The Research-Product Loop: Traditional setups isolate research teams (who train models) from product teams (who build prompts on top). Engram integrates the two, ensuring user interactions immediately and safely feed back into the model's ongoing training pipeline.
  • The "ChatGPT Moment" for Memory: The founders believe the next major breakthrough will be a model that functions like a true intern—consistently and safely getting smarter day-by-day based on local feedback, without suffering from catastrophic forgetting.

Under the leadership of CEO Miljan Gutovic, global building materials giant Holcim is transforming its traditional industry by leveraging AI to capture CHF 200 million in efficiency gains, spinning off its North American division to maximize value, and proving that decarbonization can drive profitable growth.

Global Growth Strategies & Regional Market Dynamics

  • The North American Spinoff: Holcim completed the spinoff of its North American business to unlock a new chapter of growth and capitalize on diversifying strategies between North America and Europe.   • The European Housing Shortage: Europe is currently facing a massive deficit of nearly 10 million homes.
      • Market Recovery: While high interest rates and slow permitting have historically throttled construction, a recent uptick in permit approvals and the EU’s affordable housing plan point to a near-term market recovery.

      • The Retail Model: Unlike in Western markets, nearly 50% of customers in Latin America are "self-builders" who buy bagged cement for personal home expansions. Holcim serves this demographic through its franchise network of approximately 2,500 *Disensa* retail construction shops, supplying up to 85% of their building needs.
      • Mexican Expansion: Mexico remains Holcim's primary growth market in the region, fueled by President Claudia Sheinbaum's commitment to construct 1.8 million homes over the next five years.

Transforming Materials Science & Decarbonization

  • The Scale of Concrete Consumption: Concrete is the second most consumed material on Earth after water, with global consumption reaching approximately 30 billion tonnes annually.
  • Sustainability as a Profit Driver: Rather than treating green transitions as a cost center, Holcim uses sustainability to drive higher profit margins.
      • Formulation Innovation: Over the last five years, eco-friendly cement and concrete formulations have grown to represent more than one-third of Holcim's total sales. These products replace high-CO2 raw materials with cheaper, more sustainable alternatives.
      • The Circular Economy: Holcim acts as a waste-management partner, getting paid to accept waste materials, processing them into alternative fuel sources, and using them to replace traditional fossil fuels in cement kilns.
      • Financial Impact: These sustainable practices and green product lines helped boost Holcim's European profit margins by 430 basis points over the past five years.

Harnessing AI and Operational Decentralization

  • The CHF 200 Million AI Target: Holcim has committed to achieving CHF 200 million in net benefits through artificial intelligence by 2028.
      • Predictive Maintenance: Large cement plants (averaging 1,000 by 500 meters) must run 24/7. Holcim feeds massive internal plant data into proprietary AI algorithms to detect early warning signs of hardware failure, allowing management to schedule preemptive maintenance and avoid costly unplanned shutdowns.
      • Upskilling Over Layoffs: Rather than replacing human capital, AI implementation is being used to upskill existing personnel and maximize overall operational efficiency.
  • Decentralized Corporate Structure: Holcim maintains an extremely slim corporate headquarters, operating on a "local for local" business model where countries manage their own sourcing, production, and sales. This highly decentralized structure is a key driver of Holcim's industry-leading EBIT margins.

2. Fight Complacency: High-performing teams are highly vulnerable to complacency. Leaders must actively prevent backsliding through constructive criticism, fresh targets, and peer comparison.

3. Benchmark Relentlessly: Constantly compare performance metrics both internally across different countries and externally against competitors to copy and scale best practices.

TL;DR

To unlock the multi-trillion-dollar "longevity dividend," global economies must transition from treating aging as a demographic crisis to capturing its immense potential through 80-year financial planning, systemic support for female caregivers, and a shift from reactive medical care to proactive self-care.

Redefining Financial Security and the Career Lifecycle

  • The Breakdown of Traditional Retirement Models: Kevin Strain (President and CEO, Sun Life Financial) highlighted that the traditional financial planning model is broken. Historically, individuals worked for 40 years to fund a 10-to-15-year retirement. Today, retirement can easily last 40 years or more, requiring financial plans to cover an 80-year horizon.
      • Accumulation vs. De-accumulation: Financial institutions must shift their focus from merely "achieving" retirement security to "building" it dynamically, helping clients manage assets through both the accumulation and active spending (de-accumulation) phases of a longer life.
  • The $7 Trillion Silver Economy: David Jacob (CEO, Marsh Asia) emphasized that early retirement represents a massive loss of untapped economic productivity.
      • Extending Work Spans: Creating flexible, "gig-style" corporate platforms for workers aged 55 to 70 keeps older adults mentally engaged and physically healthier, directly lowering the rates of Alzheimer's, dementia, and cognitive decline.
      • Public and Private Savings: Prolonging the productivity of older workers keeps them wealthier (allowing them to invest more in their own health) and dramatically reduces the burden on public and private health spending.

Gender Economics, Caregiving, and the Fertility Crisis

  • The Caregiving Penalty: The financial burden of eldercare and childcare falls disproportionately on women. According to the joint World Economic Forum (WEF) and Marsh report, a single year of caregiving combined with the gender pay gap can reduce a woman's retirement savings by almost 25%.
  • The "Motherhood Penalty" vs. Direct Costs: Liu Qian (Founder and CEO, Wusawa Advisory) highlighted that the economic barriers to raising children are heavily misunderstood:
      • In China, the direct cost of raising a child from age 0 to 18 is approximately $68,000 USD (extremely high relative to GDP per capita).
      • However, the "motherhood penalty"—the opportunity cost of a mother’s lost career progression and lifetime income—is even higher at $78,000 USD.
  • Redistributing Domestic Labor to Boost Fertility: Global data shows that fertility rates drop fastest in countries experiencing rapid economic growth and rising female autonomy that still cling to traditional family values (e.g., Japan, South Korea, Italy, Spain).
      • Paternal Leave Policies: True economic sustainability requires policy mechanisms like Sweden’s, which offers 18 months of parental leave with at least 3 months reserved exclusively for fathers on a non-transferable basis.
      • Economic Recognition: Incorporating non-transactional care work into formal economic metrics via "care credits" would protect women's retirement funds and structurally grow the global GDP.
      • Male Health Factors: Public policy must also address biological fertility; global sperm counts have declined by over 60% in the last 45 years due to environmental pollution, plastics, and lifestyle choices.

Shifting from Reactive Medicine to Active Wellbeing

  • The Unsustainability of Current Medical Systems: Chawalit Frederick Tsao (Chairman, TPC Group) argued that neither government budgets nor private insurance models can sustain the current trajectory of rising medical care costs.
      • From Medical Care to Self-Care: Public health policy must transition from reactive, clinical "medical care" to proactive, lifestyle-based "self-care and self-love."
      • Mindset Shift: Chronic stress acts as a systemic "death sentence" for the human body. Health ministries globally must pivot toward education that empowers individuals to take personal ownership of their mental and physical wellbeing.
  • Gross National Happiness (GNH) Over GDP: As artificial intelligence and automation assume transactional production tasks, the primary metric of a successful society should shift from raw GDP to wellbeing, happiness, and environmental harmony.

The Dual Power of Technology and Low-Cost Intervention

  • AI as a Demographic Cushion: While Western observers often warn that China’s declining population will trigger economic disaster, Kevin Strain argued that AI and advanced automation turn this decline into an economic advantage by replacing lost labor and boosting efficiency without causing mass unemployment.
  • High-Tech Medicine Extending Lifespans: Cutting-edge medical innovations are fundamentally changing survival rates. Examples include breaking the blood-brain barrier to treat blood clots using non-invasive ultrasound, and performing major lung surgeries that allow 85-year-old patients to walk within 24 hours.
  • The $6 Trillion Low-Tech Opportunity: Despite high-tech medical breakthroughs, the WEF/Marsh report highlights that the largest economic savings are remarkably low-tech.
      • Implementing simple, low-cost preventative measures—such as encouraging regular exercise, using hearing aids, and preventing physical falls in the elderly—could unlock $6 trillion USD in healthcare savings globally by 2040.

  • Valuation Realities: Hoffman compares current AI valuations to early internet-era pricing. Some will inevitably collapse to zero, while others are priced far too low compared to their future terminal value.

Shifting Defensibility and Moats for Startups

  • The "SaaS-pocalypse" Myth: The belief that software-as-a-service (SaaS) is dead is overstated. However, traditional moats built on the high cost of software development are shrinking.
      • AI-Native Imperative: Hoffman advises investors to short any SaaS company that is not aggressively transitioning to become AI-native, and to back those that are.
  • Avoiding the "Thin Wrapper" Trap: Startups that merely package foundation models into a vertical interface are highly vulnerable to first-party features from major model providers.

Instacart is bridging the gap between physical and digital retail using its NVIDIA Jetson-powered "Caper Carts," which employ edge AI and sensor fusion to provide real-time basket tracking and personalized recommendations. This "Connected Store" ecosystem is driving double-digit sales lifts for retail partners and laying the foundation for a fully unified, software-optimized grocery experience.

## The Caper Cart: Edge AI & Sensor Fusion

  • Onboard Edge Computing: Caper Carts utilize onboard NVIDIA Jetson processors to run AI models locally inside the cart.
      • Latency Reduction: Edge processing enables response times in the hundreds of milliseconds, which is critical for real-time shopping feedback. Traditional cloud processing (taking several seconds) is too slow and fails in steel-constructed grocery stores with poor cellular and Wi-Fi connectivity.
  • Sensor Fusion Architecture: The carts combine multiple physical and visual inputs to build a highly accurate, real-time picture of the customer's basket.
      • Weights and Measures Scale: The entire basket serves as a highly sensitive scale (certified for weighing produce in the US) that acts as the "ground truth" for cart contents.
      • Camera Array: Multiple cameras look into the basket to identify items and mitigate edge-case issues like visual obstruction or rapid item entry/removal.
      • Visual SLAM and Location Tracking: Carts use location sensors and visual SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) to pinpoint their position in store aisles within inches.

## Driving Retail Value and Personalization

  • Double-Digit Sales Lift: Grocers deploying Caper Carts—including Wakefern (ShopRite/Fairway) in the US, Coles in Australia, and Morrisons in the UK—consistently see double-digit percentage increases in overall sales.

  • Event Overview: The 17th Annual Meeting of the New Champions gathered more than 1,800 leaders, including 200 technology pioneers, representing over 90 geographies at the Dalian International Convention Center.

China's Economic Outlook: The Four Pillars of Growth

Premier Li Qiang outlined the state of China's economy at the launch of its 15th Five-Year Plan, defining its trajectory through four key keywords:

  • Economic Stability: China registered a steady 5% GDP growth in Q1, demonstrating strong resilience in its massive 140 trillion RMB economy despite global supply chain disruptions and energy shortages.
  • Technological Innovation: The domestic artificial intelligence (AI) sector has seen explosive growth, with daily AI token usage hitting hundreds of trillions by the end of May, ranking first globally. Embodied intelligence has also moved into large-scale commercial application.
  • Market Dynamism: Driven by green and niche consumption, China's domestic market remains highly active, handling over 550 million express deliveries daily—surpassing the combined total of the rest of the world.
  • Global Integration: Rejecting protectionism, China has extended zero-tariff policies to 63 countries and maintained its position as the world's second-largest import market for 17 consecutive years, recording a 20.5% import growth in the first five months of the year.

The Mechanics of China's Innovation Ecosystem

Premier Li rejected narratives of government subsidies driving Chinese market competitiveness, attributing success to structured, long-term national effort:

  • Relentless R&D Investment: During the 14th Five-Year Plan (2021–2025), China's total R&D expenditure grew by an average of 10% annually, making it the world's second-largest R&D spender.
      • R&D investment by enterprises accounts for nearly 80% of China's total research spending.
      • Telecommunications giant Huawei invested over 1 trillion RMB in research over nearly 10 years to bypass external technological blockades and achieve core breakthroughs.
  • Accelerated Industrial Application: China's robust manufacturing supply chain dramatically shortens the time from lab to market, bridging the "Darwinian sea" of commercialization.
      • Premier Li noted that physical blueprints can be turned into functional consumer prototypes in Shenzhen in just one week, compared to up to a year in other countries.
  • Talent and Infrastructure Development: China produces approximately 7 million university graduates in science, engineering, agriculture, and medicine every year.
      • China claims 24 of the world's top 100 innovation clusters, topping the Global Innovation Index rankings for three consecutive years.
      • Specialized hubs include a one-hour electronic innovation circle in the Yangtze River Delta and a 30-minute robot-supporting circle in Shenzhen's Robot Valley.

Transitioning to "China Opportunity 2.0" and Global Cooperation

Rather than viewing China's technological rise as a threat ("China Shock 2.0"), Premier Li advocated for viewing it as a global benefit ("China Opportunity 2.0") that offers innovation dividends and investment returns:

  • Transition to "Created in China": Global enterprises are increasingly setting up R&D centers in China, with 14,000 foreign-funded scientific and technological service firms established in 2023 alone (a 27.2% year-on-year increase).
  • Exponential Capital Returns: Rapid growth pathways exist for local tech startups; robotics firm Unitree grew in valuation from 10 million RMB in 2016 to 42 billion RMB today.
  • Commitment to Open Source: Much of China's technological advancement is designed to be shared. China's open-source AI models have surpassed 10 billion cumulative downloads globally, and its large-scale scientific facilities (including the Tiangong space station) are open to international scientists and astronauts.
  • Call for Collective Tech Governance: Recognizing the "Cambrian moment" of AI, Premier Li urged global cooperation to manage the ethical risks and control liabilities of fast-moving tech, calling on world leaders to "tear down walls and build bridges."
