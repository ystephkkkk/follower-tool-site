TL;DR

NVIDIA and Dassault Systèmes announced a significant long-term strategic partnership, integrating Dassault Systèmes’ Virtual Twin technologies with NVIDIA’s AI and Omniverse platforms to create science-validated "World Models" that will redefine industrial design, manufacturing, and operations through generative AI and real-time simulation.

The Evolving Partnership & Generative AI

  • A Quarter-Century Collaboration: NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang and Dassault Systèmes CEO Pascal Daloz highlighted their long-standing partnership, which began over 25 years ago with OpenGL (precursor to today's RTX) and CGFX (precursor to CUDA), revolutionizing workstation computing.
  • New Computing Platform: The current collaboration marks a "reinvention of the computing platform," scaling Dassault Systèmes' 40-year vision of virtual twins to represent the physical world at a much larger scale through AI.
  • Generative Economy: Industry is shifting from structured design representations (specifying every geometry) to "generative computing models" where AI is foundational.
  • AI as Infrastructure: Jensen Huang emphasized that AI is becoming a fundamental infrastructure, akin to water, electricity, or the internet, powering every industry and country globally.
  • 100% Digital Future: The future of design, simulation, validation, and software integration is projected to be entirely digital, with Dassault Systèmes' VAs (CATIA, SOLIDWORKS, SIMULIA, BIOVIA, DELMIA, ENOVIA) leveraging NVIDIA's technologies.
  • Massive Integration: This is the largest collaboration between the two companies, involving the fusion of NVIDIA CUDA-X acceleration libraries, NVIDIA AI (for physical and agentic AI), and NVIDIA Omniverse (NVIDIA's digital twin platform) into Dassault Systèmes’ offerings.
  • Unprecedented Scale: This integration promises to accelerate computing by 100x, 1000x, and soon up to 1,000,000x, enabling real-time operations for tasks like wind tunnel simulations and robot orchestration.

Revolutionizing Design & Engineering with World Models

  • Inventing the Future: The partnership empowers Dassault Systèmes' community of 45 million users, 400,000 customers, and 15+ million engineers to "invent the future," moving beyond mere automation of the past.
  • The World Model Foundation: A core concept is the "World Model," grounded in biology, physics, and material sciences, which is crucial for computing complex systems, including life itself.
      • Computing Life's Language: This involves learning the language of DNA, proteins, and cells to understand interactions and properties.
      • Generative Biology & Materials: The ability to translate between human and biological languages, generating new proteins for drugs, chemicals, or materials with enhanced properties (e.g., stronger, lighter, more heat-resistant).
      • Case Study (Bell Group): The Bell Group uses virtual twins powered by a biological world model to automatically generate proteins for healthier, more sustainable non-dairy foods, leading to faster innovation and certified decisions.
  • Infinite Possibilities & Physics AI: The virtual twin can explore an "infinite of possibilities" by fusing simulation with AI, using "surrogate and emulation models."
      • NVIDIA Physics Nemo: This physics-aware AI model simulation system works alongside or is trained by principled simulators, predicting physical behavior 10,000 times faster while remaining grounded in the laws of physics.
      • Case Study (Lucid Motors): Lucid Motors embeds crash behavior, aerodynamics, and vehicle performance early in development, allowing engineers to design vehicle *behavior* (not just shape) and certify it.

Software-Defined Factories & Industrialization

  • Virtual Twin Factories: Factories are evolving into hybrid physical-virtual assets, designed, simulated, and operated entirely within virtual twins. This allows for complex simulations of millions of objects, optimizing layouts, sequences, and robot operations.
      • Case Study (Omron): Omron engineers "software-defined factories" where autonomous elements are integrated from day one, resulting in more flexible, resilient, and adaptive production systems.
  • The AI Factory Revolution: AI is driving a new industrial revolution, requiring the rapid scaling of three interconnected industries:
      • Chip Factories: Increasing production of semiconductor and packaging facilities.
      • Computer Factories: Manufacturing supercomputers from these chips.
      • AI Factories: Housing supercomputers to "manufacture" AIs themselves.
  • Largest Industrial Buildout: A gigawatt AI factory can cost approximately $50 billion, contributing to tens of gigawatts being built worldwide – representing the largest industrial infrastructure buildout in human history, potentially worth trillions over the next decade.
  • MBSE for AI Factories: NVIDIA uses Dassault Systèmes' Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) to design, plan, and simulate AI factories (including data centers, supercomputers, networks, energy, cooling) within a virtual twin *before* construction, saving significant time and money.
  • "Looks Real, Is Real": The virtual twins offer precise digital representations, integrating industrial bills of material (BOMs) and allowing for advance validation, preventing costly mistakes in complex builds like a $50 billion AI factory.

The Augmented Engineer & Knowledge Protection

  • Virtual Companions: AI agents will serve as "companions" to engineers, augmenting the design process rather than replacing them. Every designer will have a team of AI companions trained to perform specific tasks.
      • Structured & Unstructured Data: AI facilitates the conversion of unstructured information (photos, videos) into precise, controllable 3D structured data for enhanced design.
      • Revolutionized Workflow: Workflows will be transformed, enabling rapid progression from a 2D sketch to a parametric 3D model with automatic simulation in seconds.
  • Engineers as Architects: Engineers will manage and direct their AI companions, who can explore design options, optimize parameters, and present multiple solutions for human fine-tuning. This will significantly increase the number of users of Dassault Systèmes' tools, encompassing both human and AI agents.
  • Compliance by Design:
      • Case Study (NAR): The National Institute for Aviation Research (NAR) can use virtual companions to automatically ingest and constantly verify compliance with the 10,000+ requirements for airplane certification, shifting compliance upstream to become a competitive edge.
  • From Language Model to World Model:
      • Language Models: Understand syntax, vocabulary, and "taste" in language.
      • World Models: Go further by obeying the laws of physics, understanding causality (inertia, friction, gravity), and learning through simulation and examples.
      • "Shift Left" for Design: This allows for manufacturability, regulatory compliance (integrating human values), and component approval to be integrated upstream into the design process, ensuring "good to go" designs.
  • Protecting Knowledge: AI companions will codify individual designers' skills, preferences, habits, and domain expertise, creating a personalized, private repository of knowledge that enhances individual productivity without being public or open-sourced.

Despite stable global growth numbers in 2025, Harvard economist Gita Gopinath warns that the global economy has fundamentally shifted, moving away from a predictable order due to escalating tariffs, a breakdown of trust between major alliances, and an AI boom whose financial valuations carry significant systemic risk. This profound uncertainty makes the economy far more fragile than headline figures suggest.

The Disconnect: Stable Growth Amidst Fundamental Change

  • Gita Gopinath's Core Message: The global economy has undergone fundamental changes, even though 2025 growth (3.2%) remained stable, masking underlying fragility.
  • Initial Tariff Impact: The US imposed some of its highest tariff rates in a century (actual paid rates around 14%, statutory 22-23%, up from 3%), yet anticipated severe economic consequences did not materialize.
  • Reasons for Mitigated Tariff Impact:
      • Paused Tariffs: Initial harsh tariffs were paused for negotiation, preventing an immediate shock.
      • Exemptions & Lag: Numerous exemptions for specific products and countries, alongside a grace period for goods already in transit, lowered the effective tariff burden.
  • Economic Offsets: Two key factors prevented a downturn:
      • AI Boom: Massive investment in Artificial Intelligence fueled a stock market surge, boosting wealth and consumption, particularly at higher income levels. Countries supplying AI inputs (e.g., Taiwan, South Korea) saw significant growth.
      • Fiscal Stimulus: Strong government spending in China, Germany (moving away from debt breaks), and the US (in 2026) provided crucial economic support.

Rupturing the Global Order & Rising Uncertainty

  • End of Predictable Trade: The world has moved away from a rules-based, predictable global trade order, introducing a pervasive level of uncertainty affecting businesses, especially smaller ones.
  • US-Europe Alliance Breakdown: A critical and unforeseen development is the "complete breakdown of trust" between the US and Europe, rupturing an alliance that was a cornerstone of the global economic order for 80 years.
      • Europe is now actively pursuing "strategic autonomy," seeking reduced reliance on the US for security and economic partnership, and forging new trade alliances (e.g., Mercosur).
  • Slow-Burn Effects: Similar to Brexit, the full economic consequences of these fundamental changes (e.g., on investment) are expected to unfold gradually over several years, rather than immediately.
  • Accelerated Geopolitical Risks: The current geopolitical landscape is "profound for at least our generation," marking a "once-in-a-century event" due to the breakdown of the global order, a significant departure from the post-Cold War peace dividend.

Key Risks to Global Stability

  • AI Bubble & Financial Fragility: The AI sector, a major growth driver, exhibits stretched valuations and significant potential for a correction.
      • Dot-Com Bust Comparison: A potential "dot-com-like bust" in AI could wipe out $35 trillion in wealth, far exceeding the original dot-com crash and representing a much larger share of current global GDP.
      • Profitability Concerns: Gopinath questions whether all AI companies can achieve the profits necessary to justify their current high valuations in a hyper-competitive market.
      • Financial Crisis Risk: Despite recent resilience, current easy financial conditions could be building towards a future financial crisis, potentially triggered by unexpected inflation forcing the Federal Reserve to raise interest rates, tightening financial conditions.
  • Debt Overhang: Almost all countries face record-high levels of government debt without clear paths for reduction, representing a major risk currently overshadowed by geopolitical tensions.
  • Central Bank Independence: The operational independence of central banks is crucial for maintaining low inflation and interest rates. Political pressure on institutions like the Federal Reserve poses a significant risk to this established principle.
  • Geopolitical Escalation: Threats of retaliation from the European Union (e.g., over US statements on Greenland), coupled with ongoing tensions in regions like Iran and Venezuela, risk amplifying trade wars and broader global instability.

Future Growth Drivers & Policy Outlook

  • AI's Productivity Promise: AI is expected to boost economic growth through productivity gains, though the exact timeline (a few years or longer) and magnitude (estimates range from 0.1% to 1.5% annual productivity growth) remain uncertain.
      • Job Market Concerns: Rapid AI transformation could lead to "jobless growth," a shrinking middle class, and potential social unrest if job displacement outpaces job creation.
  • Energy Transition: Investment in green energy (solar, wind, hydro) is a significant growth driver, especially for emerging and developing countries seeking energy independence, job creation, and solutions to local pollution.
  • China's Economic Challenges:
      • Projected Slowdown: China's growth is projected to slow from 5% (2025) to 4.5% (2026) and potentially 3.5% in the medium term.
      • Key Headwinds: Weak domestic consumption, an unresolved property sector crisis, an aging and shrinking population, and weak productivity growth are major challenges.
      • Required Shift: China must transition from an export-driven model to one focused on domestic demand, requiring stronger social safety nets and greater reliance on private enterprise.
  • Resurgence of Industrial Policy: Industrial policy is making a comeback globally, driven by national security concerns (e.g., chips, rare earths, defense) and reactions to existing policies in countries like China.
      • Risks: If executed poorly, industrial policy can lead to resource misallocation, stunted growth, increased national debt, and higher interest rates. Guardrails like targeted, temporary implementation are crucial.
  • 2026 Outlook: Gopinath expresses significant concern that 2026 has started with further rupturing of US-EU relations, indicating the world is "not correcting course" but rather "going off course." She hopes for de-escalation by global leaders.

TL;DR

President Trump discussed his administration's strong stance on immigration enforcement, claiming success in lowering crime rates nationwide and on the border, while also outlining his economic policies including tariffs and potential rebate checks, and his views on AI's impact, election integrity, and global relations.

Immigration, Crime, and Law Enforcement Strategy

  • Minneapolis ICE Officer Reduction:
      • Action: 700 federal officers were pulled from Minneapolis by Tom Homan, a decision attributed to President Trump.
      • Reasoning: Stated that the withdrawal was due to local authorities not releasing "murderers" and "bad people" that his administration aimed to deport.
      • Goal: To push cities to cooperate in handing over dangerous criminals.
  • National Crime and Enforcement Claims:
      • Crime Reduction: Claims crime is down in Minneapolis, Chicago (by 25%), and other major cities due to his administration's efforts.
      • Law Enforcement Backing: Emphasizes unwavering support for law enforcement (ICE, police), stating, "If we don't back them, we don't have a country."
      • Targeted Deportations: Current mission focuses on deporting "criminals, really bad criminals," including 11,888 alleged murderers, drug dealers, and mentally ill individuals "emptied into our country" from places like Venezuela and Congo.
      • Local Cooperation: Prefers to be "invited" by cities (e.g., Chicago, Philadelphia, San Francisco, Louisiana) to intervene in crime rather than forcing federal intervention.
      • Data Claim: Asserts the U.S. has "record low crime... for 125 years" and the "safest country since 1900."
      • Officer Conduct: Acknowledged "mistakes" in the Renee Good and Alex Pretti incidents in Minnesota, calling both "very sad," but defended ICE officers as "strong, tough people" dealing with hardened criminals.
  • Border Security:
      • Success Metrics: Credits policies for "historic lows" in illegal border crossings and a "beautifully closed" border.
      • Caravans: Stated that countries sent "people that they didn't want" in caravans, including criminals and those unwilling to work.
      • Kristi Noem: Defends her role, attributing the closed border to her efforts, but noted "public relations" challenges in getting credit.
  • Public Perception:
      • Poll Discrepancy: Dismisses polls showing low approval for his immigration handling, stating they are "almost dishonest" and depend on how questions are asked.
      • "Public Relations" Issue: Believes his administration does a "phenomenal job" but is "not good at public relations," failing to "sell the great job that we're doing."

Economic Outlook and Policy

  • Investment and Growth:
      • Investment Claim: States "$18 trillion being invested into the country," contrasting it with "less than a trillion" under the previous administration.
      • Outlook: Expresses confidence in economic growth, stating that growth "will soon make the debt look very small."
  • Inflation Control:
      • Inherited Situation: Claims to have "inherited the worst inflation in the history of our country" (48 years).
      • Current Status: Asserts "almost no inflation" now, citing 1.2% for the last three months.
      • Gasoline Prices: Points to recent gasoline prices at $1.99 a gallon, down from $4.50-$5.
  • Tariffs and Rebates:
      • Tariff Revenue: States that China is paying "hundreds of billions of dollars in tariffs," with "trillions" generated by the threat of tariffs, making the U.S. rich.
      • Farmer Support: Used tariff revenue to provide $12 billion to farmers.
      • Rebate Checks: Seriously considering $2,000 rebate checks for Americans from tariff revenue, confirming he "can do that" and may make the commitment.
      • Military Payment: Mentioned giving $1,776 to the military as part of his budget.
  • Federal Reserve and Interest Rates:
      • Future Rates: Confident that interest rates "are going to be lowered" and "should" be, stating current rates are "way high."
      • Fed Chair Selection: Implied his new Fed pick understands the desire for lower rates, noting that a nominee wanting to raise rates "would not have gotten the job."
      • Independence vs. Influence: While acknowledging the Fed is "in theory... an independent body," he states he knows "the economy better than almost everybody" and implies his influence is expected.
  • Government Spending and Employment:
      • Federal Workers: Claims to have "gotten rid of hundreds of thousands of federal workers," contrasting this with Democrats who "hire 2 million people."
      • Employment Claim: States "more jobs right now occupied... than at any time during its existence, 250 years."

Domestic Politics and Election Integrity

      • Local Corruption: Did not use "nationalized," but asserts "extremely corrupt" elections in specific areas like Detroit, Philadelphia, and Atlanta.
      • Voter ID: Argues Democrats oppose voter ID because "they want to cheat on elections," despite claiming "Democrat voters want it."
      • Federal Controls: Advocates for federal controls over elections if states fail to ensure honesty, proper, and timely processes.
  • Midterm Elections:
      • Trust in Results: Will trust midterm results "if the elections are honest."
      • Past Election Claims: Reiterates belief of "cheating" in past elections, despite claiming to have won "all seven swing states" and 84% of counties.

      • Warrant: Notes an FBI investigation in Fulton County, Georgia, regarding election ballots, operating under a court order signed by a "liberal judge."
      • Purpose: Believes the investigation will "find out the true winner of that state" and expose past "cheating."
  • Foreign Election Interference:
      • China: Believes China attempts to influence U.S. elections.
      • Russia: Dismisses claims of Russian interference as a "hoax," attributing it to Hunter Biden.
  • Legal Battles and Political Targeting:

      • Impeachments and Indictments: Highlights having been impeached twice ("fake impeachments") and indicted "87 different times," equating to "287 years" in prison if he had lost.
      • DOJ's Role: While he is the "Chief Law Enforcement Officer," he states he doesn't "choose to" be involved in prosecuting political enemies, but suggests that the intense scrutiny he faced justifies such actions by others.
      • Bill Clinton & Epstein: Expresses a surprising positive sentiment towards Bill Clinton and called the attempts to subpoena him regarding Epstein a "shame," noting Democrats might retaliate by targeting him if Clinton is pursued.

AI and Foreign Policy Engagements

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI):
      • Impact: Considers AI "a big deal," potentially "bigger than the Internet," with vast implications.
      • U.S. Leadership: Claims the U.S. is "leading China by a lot" and "leading everybody by a lot" in AI development.
      • Economic Opportunity: Predicts AI will be the "greatest jobs producer," "greatest military producer," and "greatest medical producer," dismissing concerns about job losses.
      • Energy Infrastructure: Proposes that new AI and auto plants will build their "own electric plant" to meet demand, as the country will need "twice the electric."
  • China Relations:
      • Positive Relationship: Describes a "very good relationship" with President Xi, with plans for reciprocal visits (Trump to China in April, Xi to White House late year).
      • Discussions: Confirms discussions on the economy and tariffs.
  • Iran Policy:
      • Threat Assessment: Warns Iran's Supreme Leader "should be very worried" due to U.S. actions.
      • Nuclear Program: Claims his administration "wiped out their nuclear" program, leading to "peace in the Middle East."
      • Military Action: States B-2 bombers "obliterated" targets, preventing a nuclear weapon within a month. Threatens renewed force if Iran attempts to restart its nuclear program at new sites.
  • Cuba Situation:
      • Crisis: States Cuba is "in big trouble" and needs "sanitarian aid," citing the cessation of financial support from Venezuela.
      • Engagement: Confirms the U.S. is "talking to Cuba" to explore options for Cuban-Americans to visit their loved ones.
      • Political Support: Notes strong support from Cuban-American voters (94%).

  • Key Regulatory Measures:
      • School Payment Caps: The House settlement introduces caps on what schools can pay athletes directly.
      • NIL Go Scrutiny: Third-party NIL deals must now pass through NIL Go, regulated by Deloitte, ensuring "fair market value" to prevent inflated payments for minimal activity (e.g., a quarter-million for a 30-minute car dealership appearance).
  • Evolving Role of Collectives:
      • Early Funding: Collectives initially filled the void by organizing deals before schools could directly compensate athletes.
      • Rebuilding and Augmentation: While some funding shifted to schools, collectives, typically backed by alumni and boosters, are rebuilding to augment school payments, with their deals also subject to NIL Go scrutiny.

  • Dominant Market Position:

      • High-Value Deals: Top athletes ("unicorns" earning over $500,000 annually) often have agents but still route deals through platforms like Opendorse for compliance.
