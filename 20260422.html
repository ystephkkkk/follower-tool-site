TL;DR

HCLTech concluded FY26 with $14.7B in revenue and 3.9% constant currency growth, signaling a strategic pivot toward "Advanced AI" which now contributes $620M in annualized revenue despite macroeconomic headwinds and softening discretionary spend in telecom.

FY26 Financial Performance and FY27 Outlook

  • Revenue Growth: HCLTech delivered consolidated revenue of $14.7 billion for FY26, representing 3.9% growth in constant currency.
      • Quarterly Decline: Q4 revenue stood at $3.7 billion, a 3.3% sequential decline driven by software seasonality and delayed procurement decisions in March.
  • Profitability Margins: Operating margin for the year was 17.2% (17.9% excluding restructuring costs).
      • FY27 Guidance: The company projects revenue growth of 1% to 4% and an EBIT margin of 17.5% to 18.5%.
  • Dividend Payout: The board declared an interim dividend of ₹24 per share, bringing the total LTM payout to ₹60 per share (97.6% of net income).
  • Headwinds: Two specific client challenges in the Americas are expected to create a 50-basis-point drag on growth in FY27.

The "Advanced AI" Growth Engine

  • Revenue Contribution: Annualized "Advanced AI" revenue reached $620 million, growing 6.1% sequentially in Q4.
  • Strategic Shift: CEO C Vijayakumar (CVK) emphasized a "New Math" model focusing on multiplying results rather than just adding headcount, with AI becoming integral to nearly every new deal.
  • Significant Wins:
      • AI Factory: A global tech major selected HCLTech for a $100M+ AI factory program to build next-generation data centers.
      • Physical AI: A semiconductor major engaged HCLTech for AI engineering services to support ASIC development.
  • AI Talent Categories: The company is reclassifying its workforce into three tiers: AI Builders (model fine-tuning), AI Superusers (3-4x productivity enhancement), and Human-in-the-loop Decision Makers.

Segment and Regional Highlights

  • Engineering and R&D (ERS): This segment led the growth with a strong 9.8% increase for the full year.
  • HCL Software: Reported $1.4 billion in revenue with an ARR of $1.05 billion. The segment is aggressively shifting from a perpetual license model to a predictable subscription-led model.
  • Geographic Resilience: North America remains relatively resilient, while Europe faces risks from rising energy prices, supply chain disruptions, and inflation.
  • Client Pyramid Growth: HCLTech expanded its high-value client base, adding one client in the $100M+ category and eight in the $50M+ category.

Workforce and Operational Efficiency

  • Headcount Growth: Total people strength reached 227,181, a modest annual increase of 1.7%, though revenue grew faster, indicating improved revenue per employee.
  • Fresher Onboarding: HCLTech added 11,744 freshers in FY26, a 50% increase over the previous year, focusing on building a cadre of "elite engineers."
  • Attrition: IT Services voluntary attrition improved to 12.5% (down 50 basis points year-on-year).
  • Restructuring Status: Restructuring efforts are largely completed, with any remaining synergies factored into the FY27 margin guidance.
